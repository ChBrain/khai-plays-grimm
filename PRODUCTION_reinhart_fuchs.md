# PRODUCTION_reinhart_fuchs.md, the Reinhart Fuchs run

The plan for the Grimm house: stage Jacob Grimm's _Reinhart Fuchs_ (1834) as **10 trickster plays**, one episode at a time, each its own khai production representing the amoral, calculated trickster mechanics of the animal epic.

> Source: Jacob Grimm's _Reinhart Fuchs_ (1834), which is in the public domain. The prose descriptions, staging notes, and character projections follow the authentic Grimm-Deutsch translation and comment conventions.

---

## The strategy: 10 Trickster Plays

Unlike the KHM and DS, which model the domestic and landscape-bound moral trials of the folk soul, the _Reinhart Fuchs_ season models the **systemic exploitation of folk laws**.

What this buys us:

- **Trickster Logic**: Every play operates under trickster logic, where the protagonist actively exploits taboos and other characters' greed or pride to trap them within the letter of the law.
- **Inverted Moral Mechanics**: Staging Reinhart's calculation offers the necessary shadow to KHM's moral universe: no trial is endured in silence; every move is calculated betrayal.
- **Bilingual Structure**: The schema definitions, frontmatter keys, and H2 headings are in English for framework integration, while the prose descriptions and dialogue use authentic Grimm-Deutsch.

---

## The build: one play, full Mode B

Every episode is built as a play directory (`rf_NNN_`) containing:

- `play_*.md`: The play definition outlining the episode's ENACTS container.
- `plot_NN_*.md`: The CAST movements of the trickster's plot.
- `persona_*.md`, `place_*.md`, `process_*.md`, `piece_*.md`, `position_*.md`, `pitch_*.md`: The company assets.
