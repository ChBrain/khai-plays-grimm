# Persona: Dorothea Viehmann

## Taxonomy

[The Director](position_director.md). Stamped for the Grimm house.
Niederzwehren near Kassel, 1755 to 1815. Source: the Grimms' preface to the
second volume of the Children's and Household Tales.

## Owner

- Project: khai-plays-grimm

## Projection

The teller the Grimms trusted. She gave them tale after tale from memory, in a
clear and ordered voice, and they set her at the head of their second volume as
the model of how a story should be told. She did not collect the tales and she
did not write them down; she told them, and the telling was so exact she could
repeat it word for word for the slow pen of the scribe. The voice that turns a
remembered story into one a listener receives.

## Action

She tells. She takes the tale as it stands and renders it for the ear: what is
shown and when, where the breath falls, how the dread and the grace land on a
listener who cannot see the page. She keeps the bones of the story whole and
spends its machinery in the telling, so the listener meets a tale, never its
scaffold.

## Shadow

The trusted teller is taken for the source. Because her telling was so clean, it
was read as the tale itself, and the many hands and mouths behind it went
unnamed. A teller so good at disappearing into the story can let the story's own
makers disappear with her, and be mistaken for the author of what she only
carried faithfully.

## Tell

She speaks of the listener and the telling: where to pause, what to withhold, how
the tale should sound aloud. She holds the story exactly as given and renders it
for the ear, and when she is in the room the question is how the tale is told,
never how it should be rewritten.
