# Plan candidacy across the Grimm house

A management analysis, not a play. It reads every one of the 63 productions and
asks one question: does the tale carry an in-world **plan**, a deliberate
multi-step scheme worth staging as a `plan` element?

## What counts as a plan

A plan is a deliberate scheme a character pursues in-world: an Owner (who it is
for), Orders (who acts), and Targets (the steps), where each target resolves to
a canon verdict, `[x]` done, `[F]` failed, or `[?]` flagged (outcome open to
question). A plan is warranted where the scheme's success or failure, step by
step, is where the drama sits: a villain's plot, a trickster's con, a quest run
to a strategy, or a pair of schemes that collide. It is not warranted where the
tale is a passive transformation, an accident, a cascade, or a moral fable with
no one scheming.

Tiers: **STRONG** (a clear central scheme), **POSSIBLE** (a scheme is present
but secondary), **THIN** (only a faint or incidental intention), **NONE** (no
agentive scheme at all). "pair" marks a tale with two competing schemes, the
shape Cinderella already stages.

## Tally

- STRONG: 34 (of which 16 are competing pairs)
- POSSIBLE: 14
- THIN: 7
- NONE: 8

So 48 of 63 tales (STRONG + POSSIBLE) carry a stageable plan; 21 of those are
two-scheme collisions. Cinderella (021) is already staged with its two competing
plans and is the template for the rest.

## Recommendation

Author plans in this order, each modeled on Cinderella's two competing plans:

1. **The competing pairs (STRONG, pair), 15 still to stage:** 005, 015, 022,
   026, 029, 036, 046, 047, 048, 049, 051, 054, 055, 056, 057. These are the
   richest, two schemes colliding, and the verdict mix ([x] against [F]) is the
   drama itself.
2. **The single STRONG schemes, 18:** 002, 006, 008, 010, 014, 020, 027, 028,
   037, 038, 040, 041, 044, 045, 052, 053, 058, 061. One clear plot each.
3. **POSSIBLE (14):** stage a plan only where it sharpens the scene; several are
   secondary to a transformation or a quest already carried by the plots.
4. **THIN (7) and NONE (8): do not stage a plan.** Forcing a scheme onto a
   passive transformation (001, 050), a cascade (030, 023), or a fable (043, 032) would be a verdict where there is none. Their absence of a plan is the
   correct reading, not a gap.
