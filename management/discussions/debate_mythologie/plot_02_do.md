# Plot: Do

## Owner

## Cue

_Pericles maps the 38 chapters of the work onto the board; Nicias examines the outline._

## Action

Nicias: If we must do this, we must map them exactly. The 38 chapters of Jacob's work cover everything from the highest gods (Wuotan, Donar) to the smallest creatures (elves, giants) and abstract concepts (fate, destiny, curses, sacred times). If we stage each chapter as a play directory (`dm_001_` through `dm_038_`), we can declare:

- The **Personas** are the mythological entities (e.g. Wuotan, Holda, Elves, Wights).
- The **Places** are the sacred loci (e.g. temples, sacred trees, wells).
- The **Processes** are the mechanical acts of myth (e.g. sacrifice, shape-shifting, swearing oaths).
- The **Pieces** are the mythic objects (e.g. the hammer, the ring, the magic wand).

Pericles: Yes. Each of the 38 chapters will become a "Cosmological Play". A play like `dm_008_wuotan` will not stage a single adventure of the god, but rather model the _Wuotan-force_ in the Grimm corpus: his roles as the leader of the Wild Hunt, the giver of wisdom, and the lord of the wind. The plots of these plays will represent the key aspects of the chapter's conceptual domain.
