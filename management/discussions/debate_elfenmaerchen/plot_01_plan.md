## Cue

_Margaret Hunt places the files for the Irish fairy legends on the table; Nicias and Pericles take their seats._

## Action

Nicias: Staging the _Deutsche Mythologie_ was a logical mapping of rulesets, but the Irish legends present a different challenge. These are narrative tales from a foreign soil. Crofton Croker collected them, and the Grimms merely translated them. Are we sure a translation belongs in a house dedicated to the Grimms' own corpus?

Pericles: It is a direct translation by the brothers themselves, Nicias, published in 1826. They did not just translate; they restructured, added a massive comparative introduction, and claimed these Irish spirits as sisters to our own domestic wights. By translating Croker, they defined the boundaries of comparative folklore. It belongs here by every right of their scholarship.
