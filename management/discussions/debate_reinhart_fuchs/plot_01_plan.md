# Plot: Plan

## Taxonomy

[Reinhart Fuchs Debate](play_debate_reinhart_fuchs.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai-plays-grimm

## Cue

_Margaret Hunt lays the old manuscript of Reinhart Fuchs on the table; Nicias and Pericles sit opposite each other._

## Action

Nicias: Jacob Grimm's _Reinhart Fuchs_ edition of 1834 is a monument of middle high German literature, but its spirit is completely different from the KHM. The animal epic is built on deceit, cunning, and calculated betrayal. Are we prepared to model a world where moral contracts are not rewarded but inverted?

Pericles: That is precisely why we must stage it, Nicias. Folklore is not a single flat moral plain. If the KHM shows the consequences of broken taboos, _Reinhart Fuchs_ shows the systemic exploitation of those very same rules. The trickster does not ignore the law; he uses his victims' greed and pride to destroy them within the letter of the law. It is the necessary shadow of our moral house.

## Stage

## Tension

Die Notwendigkeit, einen amoralischen Trickster-Zyklus in das moralische Gefüge des Grimm-Hauses zu integrieren, erzeugt eine konzeptionelle Spannung.
