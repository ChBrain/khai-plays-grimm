# PRODUCTION_mythologie.md, the Deutsche Mythologie run

The plan for the Grimm house: stage Jacob Grimm's _Deutsche Mythologie_ (1835) as **38 cosmological plays**, one chapter at a time, each its own khai production representing the mythological rule-sets that govern the KHM and DS narrative worlds.

> Source: Jacob Grimm's _Deutsche Mythologie_ (1835), definitive 4th edition (posthumous, 1875), in the English translation by **James Steven Stallybrass** (_Teutonic Mythology_, 1880–1888), which is in the public domain.

---

## The strategy: 38 Cosmological Rulesets

Unlike KHM and DS, which model character-driven narrative arcs, the _Deutsche Mythologie_ season models the **systemic laws of folk-belief**. Each chapter is staged as a **Play** representing a cosmological force or category of belief.

What this buys us:

- **Rule-Set Modeling**: Metamorphoses, oaths, curses, and blessings are modeled not as local plot conveniences, but as system-wide rulesets defined in `dm_NNN_` plays.
- **Shared Entities**: Great folk-entities (Wuotan, Holda, Giants, Elves) are defined as sovereign Personas with precise, mechanical projections.
- **Language Policy Integration**: The architectural schemas and structural labels are in English (following Stallybrass), while the prose descriptions and details use authentic Grimm-Deutsch terminology.

---

## The build: one chapter, full Mode B

Every chapter is built as a play directory (`dm_NNN_`) containing:

- `play_*.md`: The play definition outlining the chapter's conceptual domain.
- `persona_*.md`: The mythological figures and spirits.
- `place_*.md`: The sacred and cosmological loci.
- `process_*.md`: The mechanical acts of belief (sacrifices, vows, transformations).
- `piece_*.md`: The mythic tools and talismans.
- `pitch_*.md`: The tenor and undertow of the chapter's belief.
