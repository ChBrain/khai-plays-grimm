# PRODUCTION_alte_edda.md, the Lieder der alten Edda run

The plan for the Grimm house: stage Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's translation and edition of _Lieder der alten Edda_ (1815) as **5 heroic plays**, each representing the fate-bound, mythic, and tragic contracts of altnordische poetry.

> Source: Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Lieder der alten Edda_ (1815), which is in the public domain. The prose descriptions, staging notes, and character projections follow the authentic Grimm-Deutsch translation and comment conventions.

---

## The strategy: 5 Heroic Plays

Unlike the ballad poetry of AH, the _Lieder der alten Edda_ (AE) season models the **mythological and heroic stabreim songs of the Eddic codex**.

What this buys us:

- **Mythic Stakes**: Staging these plays models ancient curses, smith-trials, valkyrie oaths, and dragon-slayings under the house's strict causal rules.
- **Stabreim & Refrain**: Every play preserves the stark, formulaic tone of the original, paced by tragic refrains, warnings, and the clash of iron.
- **Bilingual Structure**: The framework schema and frontmatter keys are in English, while the prose descriptions, dialogue, and cues use authentic Grimm-Deutsch.

---

## The build: one play, full Mode B

Every Edda-play is built as a play directory (`ae_NNN_`) containing:

- `play_*.md`: The play definition outlining the song's ENACTS container.
- `plot_NN_*.md`: The CAST movements of the song's narrative progression.
- `persona_*.md`, `place_*.md`, `process_*.md`, `piece_*.md`, `position_*.md`, `pitch_*.md`: The company assets.
