# Strohhalm, Kohle und Bohne: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Strohhalm, Kohle und Bohne_ (KHM 018) as a brief etiological fable of escape and fragile alliances: three kitchen escapes traveling together across a brook where the straw burns, the coal pops, and the bean laughs so hard she splits, leaving the tailor to sew her up with black thread.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 018, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                         |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 018 | The canonical tale of the three companions and the origin of the bean's seam. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Personified Objects**: the entities are modeled as fragile physical objects that retain their material vulnerabilities (straw burns, coal pops, bean splits).
- **Etiological Resolution**: the tailor's action explains the physical trait of beans (the black seam) rather than resolving a moral struggle.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
