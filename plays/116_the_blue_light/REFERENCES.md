# The Blue Light: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das blaue Licht_ (KHM 116) as a study of ingratitude and the sudden reversal of power. The production models how a discarded servant, armed with one hidden instrument and the unconditional helper bound to it, inverts the gradient between throne and beggar, turning the king's own court against him.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 116, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 116 | The canonical tale of the soldier, the blue light, and the dwarf. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of the dwarf's service**: the helper's appearance, lifting, and fetching are treated as immediate state transitions triggered by the pipe's smoke, not as physical transport.
- **Battlefield wound**: the soldier's injury is a precondition of dismissal, not a simulated medical state.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
