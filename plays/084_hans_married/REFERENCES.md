# Hans Married: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Hans heiratet_ (KHM 084) as an illustration of how mere appearance and covetousness can carry an entire transaction. The production models the deceptive wooing in which an uncle passes off the patches of his trousers as parcels of land, the covetous father who buys the illusion, and the dreamlike dissolution of the promised riches into snow, cobweb, and glass.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 084, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857. The German declared title of the tale is _Hans heiratet_.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Coinage and economy**: the bright farthing, the milk, and the white bread are treated as a simple static post, not as a model of peasant currency or diet.
- **Material physics of the finery**: the melting snow headdress, the tearing cobweb coat, and the shattering glass shoes are modeled as a single dreamlike dissolution, not as real material behavior.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
