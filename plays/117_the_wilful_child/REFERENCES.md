# The Wilful Child: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das eigensinnige Kind_ (KHM 117) as a stark cautionary tale on wilfulness and its consequences. The production models how a child's persistent disobedience to its mother displeases God, leads through sickness to death, and outlasts even the grave as a small arm thrust up from the earth, until the mother's rod finally lays the wilfulness to rest.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 117, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                           |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 117 | The canonical tale _Das eigensinnige Kind_ of the wilful child. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Nature of the illness**: the sickness that takes the child is treated as the unexplained instrument of divine displeasure, not as a clinical condition with diagnosis or cure.
- **Theology of judgement**: God's displeasure is modeled only as the silent cause that sets sickness in motion, not as a doctrine to be argued or weighed.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
