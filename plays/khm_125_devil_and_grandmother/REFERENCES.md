# The Devil and His Grandmother: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Teufel und seine Großmutter_ (KHM 125) as an illustration of how a binding pact is broken not by force or despair but by patient reconnaissance of hidden knowledge. The production models the contrast between the two companions, who answer the closing term with mere lament, and the third soldier, who turns hope into a plan: he travels to the rock-house, hides beneath the great stone, and overhears the riddle and its answers from the Devil himself.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 125, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The exact words of the riddle**: the closing question and its three answers are treated as an abstract token of hidden knowledge to be obtained, not transcribed verbatim.
- **The seven years of wealth**: the prosperous interval is modeled as a single elapsed term rather than a year-by-year account of spending.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
