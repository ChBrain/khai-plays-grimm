# Clever Elsie: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die kluge Elsa_ (KHM 034) as a single scheme that misfires: a husband's sober test of a foolish bride. The production models the tale not as a string of comic mishaps but as one plan with a hard hinge, Hans casting a fowler's net of bells over his sleeping, lazy wife and locking her out so that her own confusion will set her straight. The net, the bells, and the bolted door are load-bearing instruments, and the outcome is decided not by the cure working but by the catastrophizing mind it was meant to mend, which dissolves Elsie's sense of self entirely.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 034, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_, KHM 034 | The canonical tale of the foolish bride, the cellar, and the net. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mere farce**: Elsie's loss of self is not played for laughs alone. It is the failed terminus of [die Glockenfalle im Korn](plan_bell-trap.md), a deliberate test whose final target closes as a failure.
- **A curable folly**: the bride's catastrophizing is modeled as the fixed flaw the plan cannot reach, not a habit a fright can break.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
