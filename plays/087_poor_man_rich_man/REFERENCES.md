# The Poor Man and the Rich Man: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Arme und der Reiche_ (KHM 087) as a moral study of two households facing the same divine guest. The production contrasts the poor couple's open-handed hospitality, rewarded with lasting blessedness, against the rich man's grudging refusal and ungoverned temper, which turn three granted wishes into self-inflicted ruin.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 087, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Theology of the Lord**: the divine guest is treated as a tester of hearts who grants wishes, not as a full doctrinal figure; his power is a plain narrative lever.
- **Mechanics of transformation**: the poor hut becoming a fine house with red tiles is a state change of the place, not a physical or architectural simulation.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
