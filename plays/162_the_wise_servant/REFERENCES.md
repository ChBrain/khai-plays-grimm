# The Wise Servant: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der kluge Knecht_ (KHM 162) as a satire on self-willed dereliction of duty dressed up as wisdom. The production models the gap between the commanded errand (finding the lost cow) and the servant's self-chosen pursuit (chasing three blackbirds), and the ironic moral that commends his "wisdom."

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 162, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 162 | The short satirical anecdote of prudent John and the three birds. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Husbandry of the cow**: the lost cow is treated as a simple errand target, ignoring any detail of livestock, herding, or how it strayed.
- **Ornithology of the blackbirds**: the three birds are modeled as the servant's self-chosen distraction (one seen, one heard, one chased), not as biological creatures.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
