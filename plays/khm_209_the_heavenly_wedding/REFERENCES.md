# The Heavenly Wedding: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die himmlische Hochzeit_ (KHM 209) as a children's legend of literal-hearted faith and deferred arrival. The production models a poor boy who hears a priest preach that whoever would reach heaven must go straight onward, walks continually straight ahead until he mistakes a great town church for heaven, feeds half his daily dinner to a carved wooden image he takes for God, and, after the image speaks and invites him, dies to the eternal wedding. The literal word carries the boy without detour all the way past the threshold of the church.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 209, one of the children's legends appended to the collection in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Doctrine and theology**: the legend's piety is staged as narrative, not as a claim or argument about faith; the production neither preaches nor disputes.
- **Material detail of the image**: the carved wooden figure of the Virgin and child is treated as a plain narrative token that grows stout and speaks, not as an object with sculptural or physical properties.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
