# Simeli Mountain: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Simeliberg_ (KHM 142) as an illustration of restraint versus greed under temptation. The production models the contrast between the poor brother, who keeps the one correct word and takes only the gold he needs, and the rich brother, whose hunger for the precious stones scrambles his mind, makes him forget the saving name, and seals him inside the mountain for the returning robbers.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 142, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 142 | The canonical "open-sesame" tale of the two brothers and Simeliberg. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Geology of the mountain**: the opening rock is treated as a binary gate keyed to a word, ignoring any physical structure or excavation of the cavern.
- **Robber economy**: the source of the hoarded treasure and the robbers' raids are taken as given; their pillaging beyond the mountain is not staged.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
