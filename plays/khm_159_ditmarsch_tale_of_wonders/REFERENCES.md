# The Ditmarsch Tale of Wonders: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das diethmarsische Lügenmärchen_ (KHM 159) as a Low-German lying tale, a catalogue of pure impossibilities recited without plot. The production models the narrator who heaps one absurdity upon another, flying roasted fowls, swimming iron, a frog eating a ploughshare on summer ice, blind-dumb-lame cripples hunting a hare, sailors drowning on dry land, until he ends by asking that the window be opened so the lies may fly out.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 159, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Real narrative causality**: the tale has no plot; the impossibilities are not events with cause and effect but a flat catalogue of wonders.
- **Physical plausibility**: gravity, season, anatomy, and ability are all suspended on purpose; the production treats each absurdity literally and does not reconcile it.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
