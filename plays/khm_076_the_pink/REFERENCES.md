# The Pink: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Nelke_ (KHM 076) as a study of patient faith and a God-given gift set against the long-running lie of a traitor. The production models how a prince born with the power of wishing, raised in ignorance by the cook who stole him, finally turns that power toward justice: punishing the deceiver, restoring the maiden he had hidden in a flower, and freeing his wrongly imprisoned mother from her tower.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 076, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of the wishing power**: the gift is treated as an instantaneous state change from desire to reality, ignoring any cost, limit, or theology of how the wish is granted.
- **Botanical detail of the pink**: the carnation that holds the maiden is a simple narrative container of her form, not a modeled plant.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
