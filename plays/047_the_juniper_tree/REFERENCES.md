# The Juniper Tree: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Von dem Machandelboom_ (KHM 047) as a collision of two schemes around a single child: a murder against a song of vengeance. The production models the tale not as a horror of the cooking-pot but as a strict contest of plans, the stepmother's design to behead, cook, and conceal set against the murdered boy's design to rise from the juniper as a bird, gather the gifts, and drop the millstone on his killer. The apple-chest, the stew, the gifts, and the juniper tree are load-bearing instruments in that contest, and the outcome is decided not by power but by who out-schemes whom.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 047, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                                |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_, KHM 047 | The canonical tale of the murdered boy, the bird, and the millstone. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Sanitized murder**: the beheading at the apple-chest and the cooking of the stew are the cold premise that sets both plans in motion, not softened away.
- **Accidental justice**: the boy's vengeance is not luck. It is the work of [das Lied des Vogels](plan_bird-vengeance.md), a deliberate plan whose steps each succeed in sequence.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
