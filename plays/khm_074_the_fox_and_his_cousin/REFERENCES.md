# The Fox and His Cousin: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Fuchs und die Frau Gevatterin_ (KHM 074) as an exploration of asymmetric cooperation, manipulative leverage, and the exploitation of naive trust. The production models the relationship between the trusting she-wolf (Gevatterin) who hopes for family solidarity and guidance for her cub, and the purely parasite-driven fox (Gevatter) who uses the godparent alliance solely to secure labor and transportation.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 074, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                        |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 074 | The canonical tale of the wolf's wife, the fox, and the lye. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Chemical composition of the lye**: the Bauern's weapon is treated as a simple scalding status transition on the Wölfin's condition, ignoring the physical/chemical properties of ash and boiling water.
- **Cub's growth and nurturing**: the wolf cub is modeled as a passive status symbol triggering the alliance; biological growth and education are out of scope.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
