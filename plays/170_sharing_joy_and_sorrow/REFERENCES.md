# Sharing Joy and Sorrow: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Lieb und Leid teilen_ (KHM 170) as a cautionary tale about the malicious twisting of a just commandment. The production models how a cruel tailor turns the order to "share joy and sorrow" with his good wife into a pretext for cruelty, and how the magistrates refuse his sophistry and give him the reward he deserves.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 170, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                 |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 170 | The canonical tale of the tailor who twists "sharing joy and sorrow". |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Form of the final punishment**: the tale closes with the judges giving the tailor "the reward he deserves" without naming it; the production leaves the sentence unspecified rather than inventing a penalty.
- **Legal procedure**: the arrest, jailing, and trial are modeled as decisive interventions of authority, not as a full procedural simulation of court process.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
