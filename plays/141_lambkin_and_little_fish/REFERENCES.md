# The Lambkin and the Little Fish: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das Lämmchen und Fischchen_ (KHM 141) as an illustration of how sibling fidelity, preserved beneath an enchantment, survives malice through the mercy of strangers. The production models the contrast between the witch-stepmother's destructive envy and the chain of compassion (cook, peasant woman, wise woman) that recognizes the human child inside the animal shape and restores it.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 141, declared in German as _Das Lämmchen und Fischchen_.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of the spell**: the witch's enchantment is treated as a single state transition from human to animal shape, ignoring any theory of how the magic works.
- **The guests and the substitute sheep**: the banquet and the sheep slaughtered in the lamb's place are modeled only as the pressure that triggers the cook's choice, not as characters in their own right.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
