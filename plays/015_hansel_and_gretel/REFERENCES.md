# Hansel and Gretel: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Hänsel und Gretel_ (KHM 015) as a collision of two schemes under famine: predation against cunning. The production models the tale not as a children's adventure but as a strict contest of plans, the witch's design to lure, fatten, and devour set against the children's design to read each trap and turn it back. The candy house, the cage, the bone, and the oven are load-bearing instruments in that contest, and the outcome is decided not by strength but by who out-schemes whom.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 015, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                              |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 015 | The canonical tale of abandonment, the candy house, and the witch. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Sanitized hunger**: the famine and the parents' decision to abandon the children are modeled as the cold premise that sets both plans in motion, not softened away.
- **Accidental rescue**: the children's survival is not luck. It is the work of [die List der Geschwister](plan_siblings-cunning.md), a deliberate plan whose steps succeed and fail in sequence.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
