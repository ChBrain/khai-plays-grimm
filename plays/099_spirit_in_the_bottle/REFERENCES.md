# The Spirit in the Bottle: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Geist im Glas_ (KHM 099) as an illustration of how courage and a single well-timed trick can master a force far greater than oneself, and how a dangerous gift, used rightly, turns poverty into wealth and renown. The production models the contrast between the spirit's raw, prideful power and the scholar-son's cool cunning, and tracks the magic plaster from a bargaining chip to the foundation of a worldwide medical fame.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 099, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Alchemical chemistry of the plaster**: the transmutation of iron and steel to silver and the healing of wounds are treated as a single magical capability, not a chemical or medical process.
- **Economics of the goldsmith's trade**: the sale of the silver for four hundred talers is modeled as a fixed exchange, ignoring metal markets, assaying, or coinage detail.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
