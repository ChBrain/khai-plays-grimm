# The Golden Key: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der goldene Schlüssel_ (KHM 200) as a study of deferred revelation and an obstacle that is a mystery rather than an enemy. The production models a poor boy who, sent out for wood in deep winter, finds a golden key, digs up a small iron chest, locates its almost invisible keyhole, and turns the key once. As the closing tale of the collection, it is intentionally open-ended: the lid is never opened and the contents are never disclosed, so the production halts exactly on the turn of the key.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 200, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857, where it stands as the deliberately unfinished closing tale of the collection.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                 |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 200 | The closing tale of the golden key, which stops before the lid opens. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The contents of the chest**: strictly withheld. The source never reveals what lies inside, and the production refuses to invent it, ending on the turn of the key.
- **Material detail of the chest and key**: the iron casket and golden key are treated as plain narrative tokens, not as objects with metallurgical or mechanical properties.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
