# The Confessions of Tom Bourke: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _The Confessions of Tom Bourke_, a tale from the Brothers Grimm's _Irische Elfenmärchen_ (their rendering of Croker). Thomas Bourke confesses his encounters with the local fairy hierarchy.

## Origin

The Brothers Grimm's _Irische Elfenmärchen_ (1826), their German translation of Thomas Crofton Croker's _Fairy Legends and Traditions of the South of Ireland_ (1825).

| Source             | Key Work / Event                        | Scope                                                               |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Irische Elfenmärchen_ (after Croker)   | The German rendering through which the tale enters the Grimm house. |
| **T. C. Croker**   | _Fairy Legends of the South of Ireland_ | The Irish legend the tale is drawn from.                            |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The fairy realm**: otherworldly figures are staged only as they act in the tale, not as a modelled mythology.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
