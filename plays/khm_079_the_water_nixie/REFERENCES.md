# The Water-Nixie: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Wassernixe_ (KHM 079) as a study of escape from overwhelming bondage through patience, timing, and the resourceful use of simple objects. The production models how two powerless children, set to impossible labor with useless tools, wait for the captor's single unguarded hour and convert a brush, a comb, and a looking-glass into impassable barriers that defeat raw pursuing strength.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 079, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                     |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 079 | The canonical magical-flight tale of the well-nixie and the two children. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Hydrology of the well**: the well and the nixie's dwelling below the water are treated as a single topological place of bondage, ignoring depth, water pressure, or how the children breathe below.
- **Magic of the thrown objects**: the transformation of brush, comb, and mirror into mountains is modeled as a fixed obstacle event, not as a simulated physics of growth or material.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
