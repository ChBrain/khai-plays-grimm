# Rapunzel: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Rapunzel_ (KHM 012) as a tale of isolation, high towers, and hair-bridge covenants: a girl locked in a tower by an enchantress, discovered by a prince who climbs her golden hair, cast out when her pregnancy is revealed, and reunited with the blinded prince in the wilderness after her tears restore his sight.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 012, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 012 | The canonical tale of Rapunzel, the high tower, and the golden hair. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Passive Captivity**: Rapunzel's tower confinement is modeled as a physical boundary that requires specific, recurring actions (the calling and hair-lowering) to cross.
- **Enchantress's Wrath**: the sorceress's punishment of Rapunzel and the prince is the logical consequence of the broken covenant between her father and the witch.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
