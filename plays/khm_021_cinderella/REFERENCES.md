# Aschenputtel: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Aschenputtel_ (KHM 021) as a systemic trial of mourning, stepmotherly oppression, and the hazel tree: a girl who plants a hazel twig on her mother's grave, receives dresses from the birds, and is recognized by the prince when her foot fits the golden slipper, while the stepsisters' eyes are pecked out.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 021, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                     |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 021 | The canonical tale of Cinderella, the hazel tree, and the golden slipper. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Fairy Godmother Tropes**: the magic is tied to the hazel tree on the mother's grave and the helpful birds, representing maternal protection rather than arbitrary wizardry.
- **Stepmotherly Abuse**: the labor trials (sorting lentils from ashes) are modeled as structural barriers designed to prevent Cinderella from attending the festival.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
