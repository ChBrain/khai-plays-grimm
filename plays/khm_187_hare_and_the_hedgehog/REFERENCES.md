# The Hare and the Hedgehog: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Hase und der Igel_ (KHM 187) as an illustration of how cunning and a hidden double defeat raw, arrogant speed. The production models the contrast between the hare's reliance on his fast legs and the hedgehog's planned deception: a look-alike wife posted at the far end of the furrow so that, at each end, a hedgehog is always "already there."

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 187, _Der Hase und der Igel_, in the definitive edition.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 187 | The Low German trick-fable of the hedgehog who outraces the hare. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Anatomy of the legs**: the hedgehog's crooked legs are treated only as the trigger for mockery, not as a biomechanical fact about running.
- **Exact distances**: the length of the furrow and the number of strides are modeled as fixed narrative constants (three steps, seventy-four runs), not as measured physical quantities.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
