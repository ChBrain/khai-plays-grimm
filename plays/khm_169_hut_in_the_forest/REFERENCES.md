# The Hut in the Forest: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das Waldhaus_ (KHM 169) as an illustration of charity that extends to the least of creatures, not only to people. The production models the contrast between the elder sisters, who feed only themselves and ignore the hungry animals, and the youngest daughter, whose full care for the hen, cock, and cow before herself fulfils the hidden condition that breaks the spell and restores the enchanted king's son and his servants.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 169, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                                   |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 169 | The canonical tale of the forest house, the kind daughter, and the disenchanted prince. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The witch's curse**: the original enchantment by the wicked witch is treated as prior backstory, named but not staged as its own plot.
- **Bird behaviour**: the birds that eat the seed-trails are modeled only as the cause of the trail's failure, not as full ecological agents.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
