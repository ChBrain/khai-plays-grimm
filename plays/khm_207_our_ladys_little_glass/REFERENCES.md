# Our Lady's Little Glass: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Muttergottesgläschen_ (KHM 207) as a brief children's legend of gracious help in answer to a freely given gift. The production models a wagoner whose heavily wine-laden cart sinks fast in the mud, Our Lady's offer to free it in exchange for a drink of wine, the little field-bindweed flower she plucks as a glass, and the cart set free the instant she drinks, so that the flower is ever after called Our Lady's Little Glass.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 207, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857, where it stands among the children's legends as _Muttergottesgläschen_.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Botanical detail of the bindweed**: the white, red-striped field flower is treated as a simple glass-shaped token, not as a plant with biological properties.
- **Theology of Our Lady**: the gracious helper is modeled only by her action in the tale, not by any doctrine beyond it.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
