# Faithful John: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der treue Johannes_ (KHM 006) as a single scheme of absolute loyalty: a servant bound by a dying charge first wins his young king the bride whose portrait has bewitched him, then silently averts the three dangers the ravens foretell, though every act of silence drives him nearer to stone. The production models the tale not as a romance but as one servant's plan carried past the cost of his own voice and life. The golden wares, the chestnut horse, the wedding shirt, and the drop of blood are the load-bearing instruments of that design, and the price of the plan is paid in petrification and redeemed only by the king's sacrifice.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 006, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                    |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 006 | The canonical tale of the dying charge, the ruse, and the stone servant. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Bloodless redemption**: the king's killing of his own sons to revive the stone Johannes is the hard close of the tale, not softened away; the children are restored in turn.
- **A counter-scheme**: there is no Gegenplan. The adversary is no second schemer but the ravens' law of silence; the whole design belongs to [die stumme Treue des Johannes](plan_faithful-guard.md), the servant's plan alone.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
