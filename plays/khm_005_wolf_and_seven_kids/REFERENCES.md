# The Wolf and the Seven Young Kids: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Wolf und die sieben jungen Geißlein_ (KHM 005) as a collision of two schemes at one door: disguised predation against maternal rescue. The production models the tale not as a simple cautionary fable but as a strict contest of plans, the wolf's design to counterfeit the mother's voice and paw and devour the flock set against the mother's design to cut her children from the sleeping thief and drown him with his own weight. The chalk-softened voice, the floured paw, the clock case, the scissors, the stones, and the well are load-bearing instruments in that contest, and the outcome is decided not by strength but by who out-schemes whom.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 005, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Sanitized devouring**: the swallowing of six kids whole is modeled as the cold midpoint that both plans turn on, not softened away.
- **Accidental rescue**: the children's survival is not luck. It is the work of [die Rettung der Mutter](plan_mother-rescue.md), a deliberate plan whose steps succeed in sequence.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
