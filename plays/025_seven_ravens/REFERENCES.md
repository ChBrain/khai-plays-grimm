# The Seven Ravens: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die sieben Raben_ (KHM 025) as a single scheme: a sister's deliberate quest to disenchant her seven brothers, turned to ravens by their father's curse. The production models the tale not as a passive waiting for rescue but as one plan carried entirely by the sister's will. She journeys to the ends of the earth, to the sun, moon, and stars, earns the morning star's chicken-bone key, cuts off her own finger when it is lost, enters the glass mountain, and frees the brothers by being recognized through the parents' ring in the cup. There is no opponent, only distance and sacrifice, and every step succeeds.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 025, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Inherited guilt**: the brothers fall through a curse provoked by the sister's baptism, modeled as the cold premise she takes upon herself, not a guilt that is hers in fact.
- **Rescue without cost**: the sister's success is not granted. It is earned by the journey and paid for with her own severed finger, the price the glass mountain demands.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
