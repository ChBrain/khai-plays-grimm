# Knoist and His Three Sons: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Knoist un sine dre Sühne_ (KHM 138) as a deadpan nonsense tale that strings impossibilities together without any claim to sense, possibility, or resolution. The production takes each absurdity literally: the blind son who shoots, the lame son who catches, the naked son with a pocket, the bottomless boat that ferries, the chapel grown in a tree where wooden clergy deal out holy water with cudgels: and lets the closing rhyme stand in for any moral the tale refuses to offer.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 138, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                 |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 138 | The Westphalian nonsense tale "Knoist un sine dre Sühne" of the sons. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Rationalizing the impossibilities**: the blind son's aim, the lame son's catch, the naked son's pocket, and the bottomless boat are staged as literal nonsense, never explained away.
- **A real resolution**: the tale ends on the rhymed couplet about running from holy water and offers no closure; the production declines to invent one.
