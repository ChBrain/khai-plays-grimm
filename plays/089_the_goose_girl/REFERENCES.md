# The Goose-Girl: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Gänsemagd_ (KHM 089) as a study of suppressed truth and patient discovery. The production models how a true bride, bound by a coerced oath of silence, is displaced by a treacherous waiting-maid and reduced to tending geese, until a watchful old king devises a way around her oath and lets the traitor pronounce her own sentence.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 089, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                              |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 089 | The canonical tale of the goose-girl, Falada, and the false bride. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanism of the speaking horse**: Falada's speech and the talking severed head are treated as a given narrative wonder, not explained by any physical or magical mechanism.
- **Provenance of the wind**: the wind the princess raises to blow off Conrad's hat is modeled as a triggered effect of her spoken charm, without meteorological detail.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
