# Donkey Cabbages: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Krautesel_ (KHM 122) as a study of charity, betrayal, and proportionate justice. The production models how an open-handed huntsman, twice robbed of his magical gifts, turns the garden's two cabbages into a calibrated instrument of reckoning and restoration: the bad cabbage punishes the gluttonous deceivers with the shape of asses, while the good cabbage releases genuine repentance back into human form.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 122, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                                    |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 122 | The canonical tale of the wishing-cloak, the gold-laying heart, and the donkey cabbages. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Botanical mechanism of the cabbages**: the transforming and restoring heads are treated as binary state-changers, ignoring the chemistry or species of the plant.
- **Aerodynamics of cloak and cloud**: the wishing-cloak's travel and the giants' cloud are modeled as instantaneous placement, not as flight to be simulated.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
