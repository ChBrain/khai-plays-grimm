# The Willow-Wren: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Zaunkönig_ (KHM 171) as an etiological bird-fable about a title won by cunning rather than merit. The production models how the tiny wren, carried unseen in the eagle's feathers, claims the crown by a trick and earns only the mocking name "king of the hedges," while the carefree lark, beholden to no king, ends up happiest of all.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 171, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 171 | The canonical fable "Der Zaunkönig" of the birds electing a king. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Ornithological detail**: the many birds of the assembly (chaffinch, crow, cuckoo, hoopoe, hen, cock) are folded into the collective will of the flock rather than staged individually.
- **Aerodynamics of the flight**: the contest of height is modeled as a simple ranking, ignoring real physics of soaring and the eagle's stamina.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
