# Frau Holle: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Frau Holle_ (KHM 024) as a moral trial of industriousness versus laziness: two stepsisters who jump into a well, serve Frau Holle by shaking her bed to make it snow, and return home-one covered in gold, the other in pitch.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 024, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                            |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 024 | The canonical tale of Mother Holle, Gold-Maria, and Pitch-Maria. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Arbitrary Rewards**: the gold and pitch are modeled as the direct, mechanical output of the characters' work ethic and obedience during their trials.
- **Underworld Portal**: the well is a passage to Frau Holle's meadow, requiring a leap of despair or obedience rather than magical teleportation.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
