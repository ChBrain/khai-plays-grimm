# King Thrushbeard: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Koenig Drosselbart_ (KHM 052) as a single deliberate scheme: the pedagogy of broken pride. The production models the tale not as a romance but as one man's plan to cure a fault. King Thrushbeard, mocked and refused, lays down his crown, disguises himself as a poor minstrel, and engineers a sequence of humbling labors (the bare cottage, the failed spinning and weaving, the shattered pot-stall, the scullery) until the proud princess's arrogance is broken and the disguise can fall. The cottage, the market, the pots, and the kitchen scraps are the load-bearing instruments of that cure.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 052, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Romanticized cruelty**: the humiliations are modeled as a deliberate pedagogy, not softened into a mere courtship game, nor escalated into abuse.
- **Accidental reform**: the princess's change of heart is not chance. It is the worked-through result of [die Brechung des Hochmuts](plan_pride-breaking.md), a single plan whose steps land in sequence.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
