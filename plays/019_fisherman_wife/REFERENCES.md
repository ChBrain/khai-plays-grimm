# The Fisherman and His Wife: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Von dem Fischer und seiner Frau_ (KHM 019) as a single scheme: a wife driving her husband back to the enchanted flounder again and again, escalating her wishes from a cottage to castle, king, emperor, pope, and finally to be as God. The production models the tale not as a morality fable told from outside but as one open-ended plan with no counter-plan, where the brake is not an opponent but the sea itself, darkening with every rung until the demand to be like God collapses the whole ladder and throws the couple back to the hovel.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 019, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                               |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_, KHM 019 | The canonical tale of the wishing flounder and the insatiable wife. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Greed softened**: Ilsebill's escalation is modeled as a single unbroken ladder of demands, not a series of separable lessons each forgiven.
- **An external judge**: nothing stops the wife but the sea. The flounder grants every wish up to pope; the limit is the rising storm, not a rival scheme.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
