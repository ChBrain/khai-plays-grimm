# The Duration of Life: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Lebenszeit_ (KHM 176) as an etiological fable that explains the seventy years of human life. The production models how God offers every creature an equal thirty-year span, how the ass, the dog and the monkey each return the years they dread, and how the discontented man inherits those rejected years as the toil, decay and folly of his old age.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 176, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 176 | The canonical fable of the thirty-year span and man's seventy years. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Theology of God**: God is treated only as the giver who assigns and reassigns lifespans, ignoring any wider doctrine of creation or providence.
- **Animal biology**: the ass, dog and monkey are modeled as bearers of dread (burden, decay, mockery) rather than as full zoological creatures.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
