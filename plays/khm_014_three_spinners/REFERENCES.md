# The Three Spinners: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die drei Spinnerinnen_ (KHM 014) as a single scheme of liberation by laziness: a girl escapes the impossible spinning task by hiring three deformed spinning-women to spin the flax in secret, promising them seats at her wedding as her aunts, so that their ugliness then frees her from the wheel for life. The production models the tale not as a lesson in industry but as one bargain run to its end, idleness turned into lasting release. The flax, the spinning wheel, and the spun yarn are the load-bearing instruments of that design, and the close is decided not by toil but by a pact in which every side gets what it wants.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 014, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **A moral of industry**: the tale rewards the lazy girl, not the diligent one; the release from the wheel is the dry close, not bent into a lesson on hard work.
- **A counter-scheme**: there is no Gegenplan. Neither the queen nor the prince sees through the deception; the whole motion belongs to [der Pakt mit den Spinnerinnen](plan_spinner-bargain.md), the girl's plan alone.
