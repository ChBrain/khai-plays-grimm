# The Sole: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Scholle_ (KHM 172) as an etiological fable on envy. The production models how the fishes' wish for order leads them to elect the fastest swimmer as king, how the herring wins the race, and how the sole's spiteful jealousy at another's deserved success rebounds upon herself, leaving her mouth twisted to one side forever.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 172, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                        |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 172 | The canonical etiology of the sole's crooked mouth and envy. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Ichthyological accuracy**: the fishes are treated as a folk assembly with speech and intent, not as biologically modeled species.
- **Mechanics of the race**: the swimming contest is modeled as a single ordered start and a first-to-finish outcome, not as a hydrodynamic simulation.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
