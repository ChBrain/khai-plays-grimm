# Brides on Their Trial: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Brautschau_ (KHM 155) as an illustration of character judgment through a simple, everyday action. The production models the contrast between three equally pretty sisters whose true natures (greed, wastefulness, measured care) are revealed only by how each eats a cheese, and the mother's counsel that selects the third, measured sister as the right bride.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 155, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 155 | The canonical tale of the shepherd, the cheese, and three brides. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Physical detail of the cheese**: the cheese is treated as a simple test object offering a measure of conduct, ignoring its dairy chemistry or culinary properties.
- **Identity of the sisters beyond the test**: the three sisters are modeled by their single act at the table, not by full biographies or rivalries.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
