# Old Rinkrank: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Oll Rinkrank_ (KHM 196) as an illustration of patient cunning overcoming a powerful captor. The production models how a king's glass-mountain trial delivers his daughter into the hands of a hoarding sorcerer, and how the long-captive princess turns her captor's own habit and beard against him, trapping him at the little window and extorting the secret of the magic ladder that carries her home.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 196, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                                  |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 196 | The canonical Low German tale "Oll Rinkrank" of the glass mountain and the beard-trap. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Geometry of the glass mountain**: the mountain is treated as a simple smooth placement that opens to swallow the princess, ignoring the physics of glass or climbing.
- **Mechanics of the magic ladder**: the pocket-sized extending ladder is modeled as a single secret key to entry and exit, not as a worked-out enchantment.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
