# The Three Snake-Leaves: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die drei Schlangenblätter_ (KHM 016) as a single scheme: a wife's betrayal of the husband who refused to let death part them. The production models the tale not as a marvel of resurrection but as a contest between gratitude and ingratitude. The young soldier earns the princess by valour, binds himself to her by a mutual death-vow, and brings her back from the grave with the snake-leaves; she answers his loyalty with a murder plot. The vault, the leaves, the wine, and the holed ship are the load-bearing instruments, and the outcome is decided not by the wife's power but by the same faithful servant and the same magic she counted on being gone.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 016, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Sanitized betrayal**: the wife's ingratitude and the plot with her father are modeled as the cold premise of the single scheme, not softened into a misunderstanding.
- **Accidental rescue**: the husband's survival at sea is not luck. It is the work of the faithful servant who kept the snake-leaves, whose recovery and revival undo the plot.
