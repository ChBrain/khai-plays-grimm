# The Turnip: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Rübe_ (KHM 146) as a comic reversal tale about merit, envy, and wit. The production models how honest labor (the giant turnip) earns true reward, how calculated greed earns only mockery, and how the threatened man turns a death trap into escape by reversing roles with a credulous stranger.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 146, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                                |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 146 | The canonical tale of the giant turnip, the envious brother, and the sack of wisdom. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Botanical detail of the turnip**: the giant turnip is treated as a single load-bearing wonder of size, ignoring the biology of its growth.
- **Logistics of cart and oxen**: transport of the turnip is modeled as a state of delivery, not as a haulage simulation.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
