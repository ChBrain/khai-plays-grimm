# Die Schlange im See: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Schlange im See_, a tale from Ferdinand Philipp Grimm's folklore collections. A fisherman catches a giant serpent in his net, unleashing terror.

## Origin

Ferdinand Philipp Grimm's published collections (_Volkssagen und Mährchen der Deutschen und Ausländer_, 1820; _Volkssagen der Deutschen_, 1838; _Burg- und Bergmärchen_, 1846), issued under his pseudonyms.

| Source                 | Key Work / Event                 | Scope                                                   |
| ---------------------- | -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Ferdinand P. Grimm** | folklore collections (1820-1846) | The tale as the "forgotten" Grimm brother collected it. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Beyond the tale**: the production stages only what the source records, not the wider regional tradition it belongs to.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
