# The Sparrow and His Four Children: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Sperling und seine vier Kinder_ (KHM 157) as a cumulative fable of counsel and faith. The production models the contrast between the worldly prudence of the three elder sons, each guarding against the particular danger of his territory, and the simple, God-trusting piety of the youngest son, which the old father blesses as the safest course of all.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 157, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Ornithological detail of the sparrows**: the birds are treated as cautionary voices, ignoring the biology of flight, feeding, or nesting.
- **Mechanics of the human dangers**: the hollow tube (gun), the snares in the straw, and the thrown stones are modeled as territorial hazards rather than as full ballistic or trap simulations.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
