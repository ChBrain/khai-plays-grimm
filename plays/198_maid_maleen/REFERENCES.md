# Maid Maleen: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Jungfrau Maleen_ (KHM 198) as an illustration of steadfast faithfulness enduring tyranny, ruin, and deceit. The production models how Maid Maleen, walled into a dark tower to break her will, digs free with a bread-knife, survives a war-ravaged kingdom as a kitchen-scullion, and stands in for an ugly false bride at her own prince's wedding, until the spoken tokens of the road and a gold chain separate the true bride from the false one.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 198, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                            |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 198 | The canonical tale of _Jungfrau Maleen_, the walled-in princess. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Engineering of the tower**: the dark tower and its wall are treated as a simple sealed placement and a barrier to be breached, ignoring real masonry, structural load, or the physics of seven years of provisions.
- **Conduct of the war**: the destruction of the king's realm is modeled only as the state into which the women emerge, not as a simulated campaign with armies, sieges, or casualties.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
