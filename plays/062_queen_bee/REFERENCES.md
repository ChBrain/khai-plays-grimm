# The Queen Bee: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Bienenkönigin_ (KHM 062) as a single scheme of mercy that pays off: a despised simpleton breaking a castle's stone-curse. The production models the tale not as a chain of lucky animal favors but as one plan whose every step is the discharge of a debt, the ants, ducks, and bees the simpleton spared from his brothers now solving the three set tasks for him. The thousand pearls, the sunken key, and the honey-tested princess are the load-bearing trials, and the outcome is decided not by strength, at which the brothers fail and turn to stone, but by the reverence for small life that alone breaks the spell.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 062, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Magical luck**: the simpleton's success is not chance. It is the run of [die Lösung durch die dankbaren Tiere](plan_grateful-beasts.md), a deliberate plan whose every target closes through a debt of mercy repaid.
- **Strength's claim**: the trials are modeled as impossible to force, which is why the strong brothers fail and petrify, not as feats a clever hand could manage.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
