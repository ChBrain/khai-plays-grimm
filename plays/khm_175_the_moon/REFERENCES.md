# The Moon: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Mond_ (KHM 175) as an etiological and comic tale of how a stolen light, hoarded by its keepers and carried into the grave, is finally raised to its rightful place. The production models the journey of the moon from a foreign oak, through theft and burial, to the heavens, and the contrast between possessive keeping and just stewardship.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 175, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                   |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 175 | The canonical etiological tale of the moon, _Der Mond_. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Astronomy of the moon**: the moon is treated as a single shining globe hung on a tree, ignoring orbital mechanics, phases, or celestial scale.
- **Theology of the dead**: the waking and quieting of the dead are modeled as simple state transitions triggered by light and by Saint Peter's command, not as doctrine.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
