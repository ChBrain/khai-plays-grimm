# Mr Fix and Ready: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Herr Fix und Fertig_ (KHM 16a, withdrawn after the first edition) as a single scheme of service and foresight: a discharged soldier wins a post by anticipating his king's wish, takes the charge to woo Princess Nomini, and on the road spares the birds, feeds the ravens, and saves the fish, so that each grateful animal later solves one of the three impossible trials set for the bride. The production models the tale not as a romance but as one servant's plan, in which every good deed is laid up against a coming task. Pipe and tobacco, the retinue, the poppy seed, the golden ring, and the unicorn are the load-bearing instruments of that design, and its reward is the rise from lackey to first minister.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, first edition (1812), tale no. 16; replaced in later editions by _Die drei Schlangenblätter_ and absent from the 1857 edition of last hand. Recorded among the tales "aus der ersten Auflage."

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Animal violence as convention**: the horse given to the ravens and the blinding of the unicorn are encoded plainly as the cost and the means of the design ([Tierdankbarkeit](process_animal-gratitude.md), [Einhorn](piece_unicorn.md)), in the conventional form of the helper-animal tale, without dwelling on gore.
- **A counter-scheme**: there is no Gegenplan. The adversary is not a second schemer but the impossibility of the trials; the whole design belongs to [der Werbungsdienst des Fix und Fertig](plan_wooing-service.md), the servant's plan alone.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
