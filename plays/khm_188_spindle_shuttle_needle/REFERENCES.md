# The Spindle, the Shuttle, and the Needle: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Spindel, Weberschiffchen und Nadel_ (KHM 188) as an illustration of how quiet diligence and steadfast craftsmanship, rather than visible wealth, constitute the true worth of a bride. The production models the contrast between the poor maiden's industrious, godly life with her three inherited tools and the rich village girl's empty display of finery, resolved when the king's son seeks the bride who is at once the poorest and the richest.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 188, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanism of the enchantment**: the self-acting tools are treated as given magical legacies, ignoring any physical mechanics of how spindle, shuttle, and needle move or weave on their own.
- **Textile craft detail**: spinning, weaving, and sewing are modeled as marks of diligence and as the medium of the gifts' work, not as technical processes of fiber, loom, or stitch.
