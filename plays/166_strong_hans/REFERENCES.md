# Strong Hans: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der starke Hans_ (KHM 166) as a strongman rescue tale: a boy carried off by robbers grows to prodigious strength, frees his captive mother, gathers two mighty companions, masters a malicious dwarf, and rescues a stolen king's daughter from the deep — only to be betrayed and then restored to triumph by a magic ring that commands the spirits of the air.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 166, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 166 | The canonical tale of "Der starke Hans," strongman, dwarf, and ring. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of the air-spirits**: the ring's spirits are treated as a wish-granting transport, ignoring any physics of flight or sea-crossing.
- **The savage count**: the count who first stole the king's daughter remains offstage, named only as the prior captor whose deed Hans undoes.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
