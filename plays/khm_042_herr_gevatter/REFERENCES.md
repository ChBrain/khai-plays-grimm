# The Godfather: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Herr Gevatter_ (KHM 042) as a warning against looking beyond one's station: a poor man who seeks a godfather, meets a strange man with fire on his fingers and horns, visits his house, and flees when he discovers the godfather is the devil himself.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 042, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                    |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 042 | The canonical cautionary tale of the devil as godfather. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Sovereign Stranger**: the godfather's supernatural nature is revealed gradually through physical signs (fire, horns, dead bodies).
- **Fatal Curiosity**: the poor man's flight is modeled as the consequence of crossing the threshold of the godfather's house and seeing the truth.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
