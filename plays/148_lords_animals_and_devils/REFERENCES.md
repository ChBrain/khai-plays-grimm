# The Lord's Animals and the Devil's: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Des Herrn und des Teufels Getier_ (KHM 148) as an etiological fable about botched creation, borrowed punishment, and a wage withheld by a clever condition. The production models how the Devil's flawed goats are disfigured first by their maker's rage, then by the Lord's wolves, and how the unpaid debt is finally collected as spite against the creatures themselves, leaving the goats with devil's eyes and stubby tails.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 148, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Theology of creation**: the making of animals is treated as a simple authorship of game pieces, ignoring any doctrine of divine or diabolic generation.
- **Calendar of the oak**: leaf-fall and re-greening are modeled as a single deferred condition that never resolves, not as a botanical seasonal cycle.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
