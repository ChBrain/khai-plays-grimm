# The Maid of Brakel: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Dat Mäken von Brakel_ (KHM 139) as a tiny comic anecdote of pious self-certainty. The production models how a girl's clearly named petition to Saint Anne meets a gruff "no" from a clerk hidden behind the altar, and how she misreads that earthly voice as the child Mary and rebukes it, never realizing the deception.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 139, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 139 | The short comic tale "Dat Mäken von Brakel" of the misheard "no". |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The golden-haired man**: the desired husband by the Suttmer gate is treated only as the named target of the prayer, never staged as an acting figure.
- **Saint Anne and the child Mary**: the heavenly figures are addressed but not modeled as agents; the only voice heard is the clerk's, misread as theirs.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
