# Godfather Death: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Gevatter Tod_ (KHM 044) as a single scheme against fate. The production models the tale not as a morality fable told from the outside but as the deliberate trick of the physician godson: where Death stands at the head of the dying bed and pronounces the end, the doctor secretly turns the whole bed so the figure stands at the feet and the healing herb may work. The magical herb, the turned bed, and the life-candle are load-bearing instruments, and the outcome is a trick that works twice and is then fatally punished.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 044, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Healing by chance**: the two cures are not luck. They are the work of [der Betrug am Sterbebett](plan_turn-the-bed.md), a deliberate plan that turns the bed against Death's own rule.
- **A Death that forgives**: the godfather's patronage is modeled as incorruptible. The trick steals from his right, and the second theft is paid for with the schemer's own life.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
