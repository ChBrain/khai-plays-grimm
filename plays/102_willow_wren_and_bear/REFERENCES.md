# The Willow-Wren and the Bear: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Zaunkönig und der Bär_ (KHM 102) as an illustration of how wounded dignity escalates into open conflict and how a small, clever stratagem can topple overwhelming brute force. The production models the contrast between the Bear's crude contempt for the seemingly least and the precise leverage of the birds' spy and sting, which turn the four-footed army's own signal against it.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 102, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                         |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 102 | The canonical fable of the willow-wren, the bear, and the war of the animals. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Ornithological detail of the nest**: the nest is treated as a topological seat of royal dignity, ignoring the biology of birds, eggs, or fledging.
- **Battle physics of the two armies**: the muster and rout are modeled as signal-driven state transitions around the fox's tail, rather than full combat or formation simulation.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
