# The Good Cloth: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der gute Lappen_ (KHM 85d) as a scheme of deception, transformation, and retribution. The production models the tale not as a moral fable about foolishness but as a mechanism in which a magic cloth is stolen by fraud, a deceiver overreaches into animal form, and the wise sister drives the reversal that destroys him. The cloth that turns rags to gold is the load-bearing instrument: it sets the theft in motion and the retribution that follows.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 85d, in the first edition of 1812.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                                               |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 85d  | The tale of the two seamstress sisters, the magic cloth, and the merchant deceiver. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The antisemitic caricature**: the merchant (der Jude) in the source text is cast as a swindling deceiver who cheats the foolish sister and is ultimately killed in animal form. This production refuses to reproduce that caricature. The antagonist is encoded solely by his function as [der Händler](persona_merchant.md), the deceiver whose fraud the sisters expose and turn back upon him. No ethnic or religious identity, label, slur, or stereotype is staged anywhere; the figure is the fraud and the false exchange the plan turns back upon, never ethnic slander to be reproduced.
- **The animal-form killing as endorsement**: the execution in dog form is catalogued as the tale's brutal structural beat in which a deceiver is destroyed by his own greed, not as a punishment the production approves or recommends.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
