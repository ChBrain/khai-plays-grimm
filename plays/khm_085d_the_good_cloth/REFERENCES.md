- **Title**: Der gute Lappen
- **Collection**: Kinder- und Hausmärchen, Volume 1 (1812 edition)
- **KHM**: No. 85 (designated KHM 85d)
- **Source Material**: Oral report
- **Digitized Full Text**: [Wikisource: Der gute Lappen (1812)](<https://de.wikisource.org/wiki/Der_gute_Lappen_(1812)>)

## Identification

- **German Anchor Line**: `"Zwei Näthersmädchen hatten nichts geerbt, als einen guten alten Lappen, der machte alles zu Gold, was man hineinwickelte, damit hatten sie genug und nähten dabei noch zu kleinem Verdienst."`

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The antisemitic caricature**: the merchant (der Jude) in the source text is cast as a swindler who cheats the foolish sister and is ultimately killed in animal form. This production refuses to reproduce that caricature. The antagonist is encoded solely by his function as [der Händler](persona_merchant.md), the deceiver whose trickery the plan turns back upon. No ethnic or religious identity, label, slur, or stereotype is staged anywhere; the figure is the fraud and the false exchange the sisters expose, never ethnic slander to be reproduced.
- **The killing of the merchant as endorsement**: the animal-form execution is catalogued as the tale's brutal structural beat in which a deceiver is destroyed by his own greed, not as a punishment the production approves or recommends.
