# Little Snow-White: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Schneewittchen_ (KHM 053) as a single deliberate scheme: the queen's murder of the fairest. The production models the tale not as a fairy idyll but as one jealous design pressed through repeated disguised attempts. The mirror's verdict drives the queen to send the huntsman for the lungs and liver, then, when the child lives, to come herself with the laced bodice, the poisoned comb, and the poisoned apple. The mirror, the apple, and the glass coffin are the load-bearing instruments of that scheme, which all but one step fails before the apple's seeming triumph collapses into the queen's destruction.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 053, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Sanitized murder**: the queen's intent to kill, the demand for lungs and liver, and the red-hot shoes are modeled as the cold facts of the plan, not softened away.
- **Accidental survival**: Snow-White lives not by luck but because the dwarfs as protectors undo each early attempt, and the apple only mimics death until the jolt frees the fragment.
