# Master Pfriem: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Meister Pfriem_ (KHM 178) as a comic satire of the incurable fault-finder. The production models the restless shoemaker whose compulsion to correct everyone blinds him to his own errors and persists even when confronted, in a dream, with a higher order he cannot fathom: the angels of heaven whose seemingly absurd labor produces the right result.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 178, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                              |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 178 | The canonical tale of "Meister Pfriem", the restless fault-finder. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Theology of heaven**: the dream-heaven is treated as a simple ordered place with a gate, ignoring doctrinal or cosmological detail.
- **Cobbler's craft**: the shoemaking is modeled only through the single mis-cut shoe as evidence, not as a full account of the trade.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
