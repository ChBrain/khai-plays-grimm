# The Strange Feast: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die wunderliche Gasterei_ (KHM 43a, withdrawn after the second edition) as a scheme of hidden menace: a blood-sausage invites a liver-sausage to a feast, the guest climbs a stair of escalating omens, is received with a friendly mask and then left alone, until a sudden voice names the house a den of murder and the guest flees, the host's knife thrusting up through the floor-hole a moment too late. The production models the tale as the gap between seeing the signs and reading them: the omens warn without speaking, and only the spoken word turns dread into escape. The broom and shovel, the wounded monkey, the knife, and the floor-hole are the load-bearing instruments of that design.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, first edition (1812), tale no. 43; held the slot through the second edition (1819), then withdrawn and replaced by _Frau Trude_; absent from the 1857 edition of last hand. Recorded among the tales "aus früheren Auflagen."

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Menace, not gore**: the violence is implicit, encoded as threat and omen ([die Vorzeichen](process_vorzeichen.md), [Affe mit Kopfwunde](piece_affe.md), [Messer](piece_messer.md)); the production stages the danger and the near-miss, not an explicit wounding.
- **No unmasking scene**: the host's intent stays hidden behind the mask of hospitality and is named only by the warning; the tale closes on the escape, not on confrontation or punishment.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
