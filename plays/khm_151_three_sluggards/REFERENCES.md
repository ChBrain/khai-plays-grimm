# The Three Sluggards: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die drei Faulen_ (KHM 151) as an illustration of an inverted standard of merit, in which the dying King makes laziness the sole qualification for the throne. The production models a contest of escalating idleness among three sons, culminating in the youngest's willingness to be hanged rather than lift his hand to cut the rope.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 151, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                          |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 151 | The canonical comic tale of the three sons and their boasting. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Medical detail of the King's illness**: the dying King is treated as a simple end-of-reign trigger, ignoring the physiology of his sickness.
- **Aftermath of the succession**: the production stops at the verdict and does not model the youngest son's later rule.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
