# The Fox and the Cat: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Fuchs und die Katze_ (KHM 075) as an illustration of practical decision-making under stress and the hubris of over-engineered options. The production models the contrast between the Cat's single, highly executed escape mechanism (climbing) and the Fox's paralyzed state of choice caused by having too many unproven strategies (the sack of cunning).

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 075, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                        |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 075 | The canonical fable of the fox's hundred tricks and the cat. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Botanical detail of the tree**: the hiding tree is treated as a simple topological placement offering safety, ignoring biological properties of leaves or wood strength.
- **Canine pack physics**: the dogs' chase and attack are modeled as state transitions triggered by the proximity of the hunter, rather than full pathfinding or combat simulation.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
