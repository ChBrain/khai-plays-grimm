# The Singing, Springing Lark: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das singende springende Löweneckerchen_ (KHM 088) as a quest-and-transformation tale of the East-of-the-Sun type. The production models how unwavering fidelity and patient searching recover an enchanted husband across years and worlds, set against the fragile condition of a curse that a single careless ray of light is enough to break.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 088, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                       |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 088 | The canonical tale of the lark, the lion-prince, and the bride who follows. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Cosmology of the celestial helpers**: the Sun, Moon, and winds are treated as oracular gift-givers, not as a physical model of the heavens or of weather.
- **Mechanics of enchantment**: the curse, the seven-year dove flight, and the griffin's flight are modeled as narrative state transitions, not as a magic system with rules.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
