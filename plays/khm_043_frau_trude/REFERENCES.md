# Mother Trudy: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Frau Trude_ (KHM 043) as a cautionary tale of willful curiosity: a stubborn girl who disobeys her parents to visit Frau Trude, sees strange figures on her stairs, is turned into a block of wood, and is thrown into the fire by the witch.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 043, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 043 | The canonical tale of the stubborn girl and the witch Frau Trude. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Stubborn Disobedience**: the girl's refusal to obey her parents is the necessary prerequisite that leads her into the witch's house.
- **Object Transformation**: turning the girl into a block of wood is the witch's method of absorbing her into the household fuel.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
