# The Two Brothers: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die zwei Brüder_ (KHM 060) as a single usurper's scheme that overreaches: a coward marshal stealing a hero's deed. The production models the tale not as a sprawling romance of twins, beasts, and witches but with one load-bearing plan at its core, the marshal beheading the sleeping dragon-slayer, seizing the dragon-heads as false proof, and coercing the rescued princess into naming him her savior. The knife, the severed heads, and the missing tongues are the instruments, and the outcome is decided not by the marshal's force but by the seven tongues he never thought to take, which expose the theft at the wedding feast.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 060, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Impostor's luck**: the marshal's near-success is not chance. It is the run of [der erschlichene Drachenruhm](plan_false-claim.md), a deliberate scheme whose targets close one by one before the final failure.
- **The proof that holds**: the false claim is modeled as a theft that omits the one true token, the tongues, not a lie that could have stood on its own.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
