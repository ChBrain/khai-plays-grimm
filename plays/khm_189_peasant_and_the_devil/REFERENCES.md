# The Peasant and the Devil: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Bauer und der Teufel_ (KHM 189) as an illustration of far-sighted cunning defeating raw greed. The production models how a clever peasant exploits the Devil's ignorance of where value grows, agreeing first to keep what grows below ground and then what grows above, so that whichever half the Devil claims he is left with the worthless one and the buried treasure passes entirely to the peasant.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 189, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                           |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 189 | The canonical fable of the peasant who twice outwits the Devil. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Agronomic detail of the crops**: turnips and wheat are treated as simple above-ground and below-ground placements of value, ignoring real soil, weather, or yield mechanics.
- **Theology of the Devil**: the Devil is modeled only as a small black creature guarding treasure and bound by a bargain, not as a metaphysical force; his fury is a state transition into the rocks.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
