# Herr Korbes: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Herr Korbes_ (KHM 041) as a single ambush scheme of small things. The production models the tale not as a string of nonsense mishaps but as the deliberate plan of the cock and hen: gather a band on the road, infiltrate the wicked man's empty house, and station each member as a hidden trap so that the homecoming villain is driven from blow to blow into his death. The carriage, the egg, the needles, and above all the millstone are load-bearing instruments, and the outcome is decided by combined position, not by strength.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 041, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                          |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_, KHM 041 | The canonical tale of the gathered band and the deadly ambush. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Random slapstick**: the attacks are not chance mishaps. Each is a stationed trap, the work of [der Hinterhalt im Korbes-Haus](plan_ambush-korbes.md), a deliberate plan whose posts trigger in sequence.
- **A villain we pity**: Herr Korbes is modeled as the wicked target the scheme is built against, and his death is the plan's verdict, not a tragedy.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
