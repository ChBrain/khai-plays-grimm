# God: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging chapter 2 of the _Deutsche Mythologie_, "God", as a cosmological ruleset rather than a narrative. The production models the transition from a polytheistic order - many gods for many powers of nature - into a single monotheistic concept that absorbs the old forces, and the fading of the old names, some surviving only in the days of the week.

## Origin

Jacob Grimm's _Deutsche Mythologie_ (1835), chapter 2, in the definitive 4th edition (posthumous, 1875), through James Steven Stallybrass's public-domain translation _Teutonic Mythology_ (1880-1888).

| Source          | Key Work / Event                      | Scope                                                                     |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Jacob Grimm** | _Deutsche Mythologie_, ch. 2 ("Gott") | The scholarly account of the Germanic concept of god in transition.       |
| **Stallybrass** | _Teutonic Mythology_ (translation)    | The public-domain English rendering the house's structural labels follow. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Narrative arc**: this is a ruleset, not a tale; no character-driven plot is staged. The plots model conceptual stages, not events.
- **Doctrine**: the production models folk-belief as Grimm reports it, taking no theological position on the beliefs described.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
