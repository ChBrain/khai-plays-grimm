# The Cunning Little Tailor: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Vom klugen Schneiderlein_ (KHM 114) as an illustration of how cunning and prepared wit defeat pride, brute strength, and envy. The production models the contrast between the proud princess's riddle and the lowly tailor's bold answer, and between the bear's raw power and the tailor's foresight, by which the humblest suitor wins both crown and contentment.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 114, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                              |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 114 | The canonical tale of the clever tailor, the riddle, and the bear. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The two elder tailors**: the rival brothers are treated as a brief failing foil within the riddle and procession plots, not given their own persona or interior life.
- **Carriage mechanics**: the bridal procession's coach is rendered as a simple conveyance from castle to church, ignoring any physical detail of horses or road.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
