# The Death of the Little Hen: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Von dem Tode des Hühnchens_ (KHM 080) as a cumulative chain tale in which a single small act of greed propagates into a cascade of deaths. The production models how the hen's choking triggers a chain of conditional exchanges for water that arrives too late, and how the unchecked grief that follows draws the mice, the forest beasts, and finally the faithful cock into a runaway sequence of drownings until no one is left.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 080, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Botanical detail of the nut and willow**: the nut and the bride's willow are treated as plot tokens, ignoring the biology of the kernel or the wood.
- **Bridge physics**: the failures of straw, coal, and stone are modeled as scripted state transitions of the crossing, not as fluid dynamics or material simulation.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
