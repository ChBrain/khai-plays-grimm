# The Golden Goose: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die goldene Gans_ (KHM 064) as a systemic validation of the contract of generosity versus the magnet of greed. The production models the tale not as a series of lucky accidents but as a mechanical chain reaction: sharing a dry ash-cake in the forest triggers the gift of the golden goose, which in turn acts as a physical trap that binds the greedy and curious one-by-one. The princess's laughter is the turning point that forces the king to set three physical tests of capacity (the cellar, the mountain, the ship) resolved by the dwarf's companions who carry the exact capacity needed.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 064, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                          |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 064 | The canonical tale of the sharing test and the adhesive goose. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Absurd Laughter**: the princess's laughter is not treated as a psychological state but as the structural key that unlocks the royal contract, resolving the initial condition of the town.
- **Limitless Capacity**: the consumption of the wine and bread is modeled as a matching of resources (the dwarf's companions who are infinitely hungry/thirsty) rather than a human magic feat.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
