# Clever Gretel: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das kluge Gretel_ (KHM 077) as a comic illustration of self-serving deception and the exploitation of misunderstanding. The production models how a cook covers her own gluttony by playing two trusting men against each other, turning the innocent sound of a whetted knife into false proof of a deadly threat.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 077, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                      |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 077 | The canonical farce of the cook who eats the roast and lies her way clear. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Culinary detail of the roast**: the two fowls are treated as a simple object of temptation and proof, ignoring the chemistry of roasting or the carving itself.
- **Geography of the flight**: the guest's escape down the street is modeled as a single state transition triggered by fear, not as full pathfinding through the town.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
