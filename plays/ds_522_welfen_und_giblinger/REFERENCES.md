# Welfen und Giblinger: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Welfen und Giblinger_ (DS 522) as a study of how a battle cry is born and hardens into a party name. The production models the field near Neresheim, where Friedrich von Schwaben's victory over the Bavarians turns two shouts - "Hie Welf!" against "Hier Gibling!" - into the standing banners of the losing and the winning side.

## Origin

The Brothers Grimm's _Deutsche Sagen_ (1816-1818), legend no. 522.

| Source             | Key Work / Event         | Scope                                                           |
| ------------------ | ------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Deutsche Sagen_, DS 522 | The legend deriving the Welf/Gibling war cries from the battle. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The historical feud**: the wider Welf-Hohenstaufen conflict is out of frame; the production stages only the legend's account of the cries' origin as Grimm records it.
- **The battle itself**: the clash at Neresheim is a precondition for the shouts, not a simulated engagement.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
