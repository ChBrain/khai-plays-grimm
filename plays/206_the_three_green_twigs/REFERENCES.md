# The Three Green Twigs: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die drei grünen Zweige_ (KHM 206) as a study of judgment, grace withdrawn, and penance. The production models a pious hermit who is daily served by an angel until, seeing a condemned criminal led to the gallows, he judges him harshly in his heart and at once loses the angel. A little bird reveals his sin, the angel returns with a dry branch, and the hermit must wander and beg, never sleeping twice in the same place, until three green twigs sprout from the dead wood, which they do the night his penance moves three robbers to repentance and he dies.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 206, one of the Children's Legends (Kinder-Legenden) that close the collection, here titled in German "Die drei grünen Zweige".

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The crime of the condemned man**: strictly withheld. The source never says what he did, and the production refuses to invent it, treating him only as the occasion of the hermit's judgment.
- **The theology of grace**: the angel's coming and going are treated as plain narrative tokens of grace given and withdrawn, not as a doctrinal system to be argued.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
