# The Poor Miller's Boy and the Cat: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der arme Müllersbursch und das Kätzchen_ (KHM 106) as an illustration of how patient, faithful service and humility raise the despised underdog above the self-satisfied clever. The production models the contrast between the two apprentices' calculating cleverness, which yields only a blind and a lame horse, and Hans's arglos loyalty, which earns the enchanted cat's disenchantment, the finest horse, the mill, and the princess.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 106, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of the enchantment**: the spell binding the princess into a cat is treated as a given condition lifted by completed service, not as a magical system to be simulated.
- **Metallurgy of the silver tools**: the silver axe, wedge, saw, and golden scythe are modeled as emblems of the bound service, ignoring any physical property of the metals.
