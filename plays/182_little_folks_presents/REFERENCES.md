# The Little Folks' Presents: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Geschenke des kleinen Volkes_ (KHM 182) as a moral fable of contentment against greed. The production models a single supernatural gift granted under one rule (take only a modest measure), contrasting the content tailor whose coal turns to lasting gold with the greedy goldsmith whose hoarding voids both the new gift and the first, leaving him bald and burdened with a second hump.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 182, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                            |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 182 | The canonical tale of the little folk's coal that turns to gold. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of the transformation**: the change of coal into gold is treated as a moral verdict bound to measure, not as a chemical or alchemical process to be simulated.
- **Anatomy of the hump**: the second hump and permanent baldness are modeled as fixed marks of the verdict, ignoring any medical or physical cause.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
