# Allerleirauh: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Allerleirauh_ (KHM 065) as a systemic representation of camouflage, identity preservation, and social ascent. The production models the princess's trajectory from persecution (forced by the father's oath) to exile, kitchen labor, and gradual revelation through golden tokens dropped in the young king's soup. The dresses (sun, moon, stars) and the fur mantle are treated not merely as clothes but as the physical interfaces of her identity transition.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 065, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                          |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 065 | The canonical tale of the girl in the fur mantle and the soup. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Psychological Incest Dynamics**: the father's forced proposal is treated strictly as a narrative boundary condition (the trigger of the flight) and a breakdown of local order.
- **Instant Magic Transformations**: the dressing and cleaning are modeled as discrete state transitions (Tarnung and Enthüllung) requiring physical labor and private spaces.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
