# Sweet Porridge: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der süße Brei_ (KHM 103) as an illustration of an unbounded resource and the single condition that keeps it safe. The production models the contrast between a gift that perfectly solves scarcity (the pot that cooks sweet porridge on command) and the catastrophe that follows when the one limiting instruction (the stopping word) is forgotten.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 103, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 103 | The canonical tale "Der süße Brei" of the pot that floods a town. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Fluid dynamics of the porridge**: the overflow is treated as an unbounded fill that spreads room by room, ignoring viscosity, flow rate, or volume calculation.
- **Townsfolk response**: the inhabitants of the flooded town are abstracted away; only the obligation to eat through the porridge is recorded, not their actions.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
