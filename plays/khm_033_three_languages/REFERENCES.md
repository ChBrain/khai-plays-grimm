# Die drei Sprachen: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die drei Sprachen_ (KHM 033) as a spiritual trial of seemingly worthless education: a boy who learns only the languages of dogs, birds, and frogs, cast out by his father, who uses these tongues to heal the sick, quiet barking dogs, and ultimately be elected Pope.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 033, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                   |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 033 | The canonical tale of the youth who understood animals and became Pope. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Worthless Knowledge**: the father's rejection is the initial barrier; the seemingly useless languages are the precise tools that resolve subsequent trials.
- **Papal Election**: the bird's selection of the boy is modeled as a natural alignment of his unique linguistic capacity rather than a divine miracle.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
