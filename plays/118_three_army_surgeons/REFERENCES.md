# The Three Army-Surgeons: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die drei Feldscherer_ (KHM 118) as a macabre comedy of hubris and its unintended cost. The production models how three surgeons' boastful self-mutilation, undone by a careless night and a thieving cat, ends in an irreversible swap of their own limbs for foreign ones, and how an innocent innkeeper is forced to pay for another's recklessness.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 118, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                  |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 118 | The canonical tale of the three army-surgeons and the swapped members. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Medical mechanism of the salve**: the wonder-salve is treated as an opaque reattachment device, ignoring any physiology of healing or grafting.
- **Bodily detail of dismemberment**: the cutting of hand, heart, and eyes is modeled as a discrete state change of the plate's contents, not as anatomy or pain.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
