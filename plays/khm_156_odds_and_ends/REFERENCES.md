# Odds and Ends: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Schlickerlinge_ (KHM 156) as an illustration of how carelessly discarded value is reclaimed by diligence and how idle pride exposes itself. The production models the contrast between the lazy maiden who throws away the flax scraps (the "odds and ends") and the industrious servant who gathers, cleans, and spins them into a gown that wins her the bridegroom.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 156, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 156 | The canonical moral tale of the discarded flax and the two maids. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Craft detail of spinning**: the flax, cleaning, and spinning are treated as a single value-bearing transformation, ignoring the mechanics of the wheel or the chemistry of fibre.
- **Courtship and ceremony**: the wooing and wedding are modeled as state transitions toward the choice of bride, rather than full social ritual.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
