# Hans the Hedgehog: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Hans mein Igel_ (KHM 108) as an illustration of the animal-bridegroom tale: a child born half hedgehog and half boy who, scorned and set behind the stove, builds his own fortune in the forest and wins both a kingdom and his human shape by holding parties to the binding force of a written word. The production models the contrast between the first king and princess, who break the promised bond and are bloodied for it, and the second king and princess, whose kept word opens the path to disenchantment and inheritance.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 108, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                  |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 108 | The canonical tale of _Hans mein Igel_, the hedgehog-boy who is freed. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Anatomy of the hybrid body**: Hans being hedgehog above and boy below is treated as a fixed enchanted condition, not a biological mechanism to be explained.
- **Mechanics of disenchantment**: the burning of the skin and the physician's salves are modeled as a state transition from cursed to redeemed, not as a chemistry of healing.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
