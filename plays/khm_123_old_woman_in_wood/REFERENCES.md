# The Old Woman in the Wood: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Alte im Wald_ (KHM 123) as a disenchantment tale about humble trust and faithful obedience. The production models how a forsaken servant girl, provided for by a white dove through golden keys, breaks an enchantress's spell by fetching a single plain ring, thereby freeing the prince and his retinue from the trees that held them.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 123, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                        |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 123 | The canonical tale of the orphaned girl, the white dove, and the plain ring. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of enchantment**: the transformation of prince, servants, and horses into trees is treated as a state binding broken by the ring, not a magical system to be simulated.
- **The robbers' fate**: the bandits who kill the travelling party serve only as the inciting threat and are not tracked beyond the ambush.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
