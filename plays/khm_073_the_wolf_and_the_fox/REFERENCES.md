# The Wolf and the Fox: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Wolf und der Fuchs_ (KHM 073) as a representation of asymmetric power dynamics, physical versus mental leverage, and the constraints of greed. The production models the relationship between the domineering Wolf (representing brute force) and the Fox (representing strategic planning and boundary awareness). The resolution demonstrates how the Wolf's inability to calculate his own physical volume (due to gluttony) becomes a fatal boundary constraint, while the Fox's constant measurement of the exit hole ensures his survival.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 073, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 073 | The canonical tale of the wolf, the fox, and the farmer's cellar. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Farming community detail**: the peasants and farm owners are modeled purely as property defenders (the beaters/slayers) rather than distinct characters.
- **Physical rate of digestion**: the wolf's increase in physical volume is treated as a simple state transition triggered by eating meat, rather than active digestive physics.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
