# The Two Kings' Children: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _De beiden Künigeskinner_ (KHM 113) as a magical-flight tale of the Master-Maid type: a prophesied prince is carried off by an enchanted stag, survives impossible conditions only through the youngest king's daughter and her knotted handkerchief, and, after a forgetting-kiss erases his memory, is won back by the patient return of his true bride. The production models how faithful, resourceful patience prevails over enchantment, broken promises, and enforced forgetting.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 113, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                   |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 113 | The Low German tale "De beiden Künigeskinner", the two kings' children. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of the earth-men**: the spirits summoned by the knotted handkerchief are treated as an instantaneous completion of any set task, not as a simulated labour force.
- **Theology of the saint**: the stone Saint Christopher figures answer for the sleeping watcher as a plot device; their religious significance is left to the source and not modeled.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
