# The Three Brothers: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die drei Brüder_ (KHM 124) as an illustration of how perfected craft earns its reward while fraternal love outlasts any inheritance. The production models a fair contest of skill (the apprenticeship and the three feats) against the bond of the brothers, who stay together past the verdict and into a single grave.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 124, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                  |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 124 | The canonical fable of the three brothers' trades and their one grave. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Physics of the feats**: the shaving hare, the re-shod gallop, and the dry blade are treated as instantaneous demonstrations of skill, not as kinematic simulations.
- **Estate law**: the father's bequest is modeled as a single authoritative verdict, ignoring formal inheritance procedure.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
