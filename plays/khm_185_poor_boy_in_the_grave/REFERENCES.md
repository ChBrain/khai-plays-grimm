# The Poor Boy in the Grave: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der arme Junge im Grab_ (KHM 185) as a study of innocence destroyed by cruelty. The production models how a guileless orphan, mistreated by a rich farmer and his wife, mistakes harmless honey and wine for poison and dies in an open grave, while the cruelty of his masters rebounds upon themselves in fire, poverty, and remorse.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 185, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                      |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 185 | The canonical tale of the orphaned boy who dies in the churchyard's grave. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Theology of suicide and salvation**: the boy's death is staged as the tale tells it, without doctrinal judgment of his despairing act.
- **Forensic cause of death**: the killing combination of strong wine and cold night-dew is treated as a narrative fact, not a medical simulation.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
