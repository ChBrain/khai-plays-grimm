# Briar Rose: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Dornröschen_ (KHM 050) as a temporal trial of a hundred-year sleep: a princess cursed by a slighted wise woman to prick her finger on a spindle, fell the entire castle into a deep sleep behind a hedge of thorns, and is awakened by the fated prince at the exact hour of her release.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 050, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 050 | The canonical tale of Sleeping Beauty and the hundred-year sleep. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Fated Princes**: the death of the early princes in the thorn hedge is modeled as the consequence of attempting to enter before the hundred-year cycle has elapsed.
- **Wise Women Covenants**: the curse is the execution of a spindle curse, driven by the thirteenth wise woman's anger.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
