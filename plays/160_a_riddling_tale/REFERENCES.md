# A Riddling Tale: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Rätselmärchen_ (KHM 160) as an illustration of identification under perfect symmetry: three enchanted maidens grow as identical flowers, and the production models how a single incidental mark (the absence of dew) breaks the tie and enables recognition and redemption.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 160, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                            |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 160 | The canonical riddle of the three identical flowers and the dew. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Nature of the enchantment**: the spell that turns the maidens into flowers is treated as a given precondition, not a mechanism to be simulated.
- **Botanical detail of the flowers**: the flowers are modeled as identical tokens distinguished only by the dew, ignoring species or growth.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
