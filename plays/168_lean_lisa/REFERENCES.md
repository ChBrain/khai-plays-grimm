# Lean Lisa: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die hagere Liese_ (KHM 168) as a short comic study of restless greed: a woman who toils herself thin and, lying sleepless in bed, builds an entire fortune out of coins she does not yet have and a cow she has not yet bought, until a quarrel over its not-yet-given milk ends in a scuffle and an open shrug from the narrator.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 168, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                            |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 168 | The comic tale of lean Lisa, long Laurence, and the dreamed cow. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The contrasting idlers**: Lazy Harry and Fat Trina are named only as opposite temperaments and never enter the scene; the production keeps them off-stage as mere foils.
- **Economic plausibility**: the chain from three imagined coins to a fattened calf is modeled as Lisa's fantasy, not as a sound calculation; its arithmetic is left untested.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
