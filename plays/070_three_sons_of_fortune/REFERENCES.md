# The Three Sons of Fortune: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die drei Glückskinder_ (KHM 070) as a representation of isolation, relative value, and communication breakdown. The production models three distinct closed worlds where basic tools (timekeeping, agriculture, pest control) are unknown. The resolution of each son's quest demonstrates how value is relative to local scarcity. The final segment models the cat's meowing as an unintelligible signal that triggers systemic panic and defensive escalation (bombardment).

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 070, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 070 | The canonical tale of the three sons, the tools, and the cat. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Economic Arbitrage Calculations**: the exact market rates of gold or horses/mules are simplified to qualitative wealth states (gold-laden beasts).
- **Cat Psychology**: the cat's behavior is modeled strictly as a sequence of simple state transitions (mouse-catcher, thirsty-vocalizer) rather than internal agency.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
