# The Robber Bridegroom: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Raeuberbraeutigam_ (KHM 040) as a single survival scheme under the threat of marriage. The production models the tale not as a passive horror but as the deliberate plan of the miller's daughter: she enters the murderer's house to leave it alive and to walk out with proof. The ashes, the strewn peas and lentils, the cask she hides behind, and the severed ring-finger are load-bearing instruments, and the outcome is decided at the wedding feast, where the crime told as a "dream" becomes an accusation no one can deny.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 040, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                                                        |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_, KHM 040 | The canonical tale of the forest house, the witnessed murder, and the exposure at the feast. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Passive victimhood**: the bride is not a helpless target. Her survival is the work of [die List der Braut](plan_bride-survival.md), a deliberate plan whose steps each succeed in sequence.
- **Accidental escape**: the return is not luck. It is secured in advance by the doubled trail of ashes and of strewn peas and lentils.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
