# Old Sultan: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der alte Sultan_ (KHM 048) as a collision of two schemes between an old dog and a hungry wolf: a staged rescue against a claim for repayment. The production models the tale not as a barnyard fable but as a strict contest of plans, Sultan's design to fake a kidnapping and win back his place set against the wolf's design to collect a sheep for the favor and duel when refused. The child, the bacon-soup, and the field are load-bearing instruments in that contest, and the outcome is decided not by teeth but by who out-schemes whom.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 048, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                               |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_, KHM 048 | The canonical tale of the old dog, the wolf, and the staged rescue. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Sanitized desperation**: the death-sentence on a toothless dog is the cold premise that sets both plans in motion, not softened away.
- **Accidental peace**: the wolf's defeat and the final truce are not luck. They are the work of the dog's [Pflichttreue](process_defense.md) and the comic confusion of [die Forderung des Wolfs](plan_wolf-claim.md), whose steps fail and patch in sequence.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
