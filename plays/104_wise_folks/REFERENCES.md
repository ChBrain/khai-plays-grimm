# Wise Folks: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die klugen Leute_ (KHM 104), the noodlehead tale in which the peasant Hans, exasperated by his foolish wife Trina, sets out to find folk even sillier than she is. The production models the irony of "wisdom" defined only by comparison: Hans is clever solely because he can locate and exploit a deeper credulity in others, recovering through their folly what his wife lost to a swindler.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 104, "Die klugen Leute", in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Theology of heaven**: the woman's belief in a reachable heaven is treated as a plot lever of credulity, not as a doctrinal claim to be examined or judged.
- **Economics of the cattle trade**: the price of two hundred thalers and the swindle are modeled as fixed narrative tokens, not as a market simulation of livestock value.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
