# The Six Swans: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die sechs Schwaene_ (KHM 049) as a collision of two schemes around a woman who must not speak: a silent curse-breaking against a slander that weaponizes that silence. The production models the tale not as a passive ordeal but as a strict contest of plans, the sister's design to hold six years of silence and sew six shirts set against the queen-mother's design to steal each newborn and frame the mute queen as a child-eater. The shirts, the swan-skins, the unfinished sleeve, and the pyre are load-bearing instruments in that contest, and the outcome is decided not by argument but by which plan outlasts the other to the fire.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 049, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                                       |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_, KHM 049 | The canonical tale of the swan-brothers, the silent sister, and the shirts. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Sanitized ordeal**: the stolen newborns, the bloodied mouth, and the death-sentence are the cold premise that sets both plans in motion, not softened away.
- **Accidental vindication**: the queen's rescue is not luck. It is the work of [das schweigende Naehen](plan_silent-sewing.md), a deliberate plan whose silence holds and whose shirts all but complete in sequence.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
