# Marienkind: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Marienkind_ (KHM 003) as a drama of forbidden entry, silence, and grace: a girl taken to Heaven by the Virgin Mary who opens the forbidden thirteenth door, lies about it, is returned to Earth mute, and is saved from the stake at the final moment when she confesses her guilt.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 003, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                        |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 003 | The canonical tale of the Virgin Mary's foster child and the forbidden door. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Sensationalist Martyrdom**: the threat of the stake is modeled strictly as a structural consequence of the girl's persistent denial, not as a religious spectacle.
- **Silence as Punishment**: her muteness is the physical manifestation of her refusal to confess her transgressions, which is only lifted upon her honest confession.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
