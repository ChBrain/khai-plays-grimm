# The Four Skilful Brothers: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die vier kunstreichen Brüder_ (KHM 129) as an illustration of complementary specialization: four sons each master one craft and one magic tool, and the rescue of the king's daughter from the dragon succeeds only through the exact, interlocking sequence of their four arts. The production models how no single skill suffices and how a just ruler rewards an indivisible joint achievement with equal shares.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 129, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 129 | The canonical tale of the thief, stargazer, huntsman, and tailor. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of the magic tools**: the telescope, never-missing gun, and invisible needle are treated as perfect, deterministic powers, ignoring optics, ballistics, or stitchery.
- **Seafaring and navigation**: the voyage to the dragon's rock is modeled as a single transit, not a simulation of winds, currents, or sailing craft.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
