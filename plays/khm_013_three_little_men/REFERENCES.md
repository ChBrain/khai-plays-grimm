# The Three Little Men in the Wood: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die drei Maennlein im Walde_ (KHM 013) as a single scheme of envy that fails at every turn. The production models the tale not as a simple reward-and-punishment fable but as one stepmother's two-stage design to be rid of her beautiful stepdaughter and raise her own ugly child: first the freezing errand for strawberries in a paper dress, then the drowning of the married queen and the substitution of her daughter in the royal bed. The paper dress, the strawberries, and the sword are the load-bearing instruments, and the gnomes' blessing turns every move back on the schemer.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 013, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                                  |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_, KHM 013 | The canonical tale of the gnomes, the paper dress, and the duck queen. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Reward as luck**: the stepdaughter's survival and rise are modeled as the gnomes' deliberate blessing earned by humility, set against the stepmother's deliberate scheme, not as random fortune.
- **Bloodless villainy**: the freezing errand and the drowning are modeled as real murder attempts, the steps of [die Verdraengung der Stieftochter](plan_stepmother-supplanting.md), not softened away.
- **The substitution undetected**: the ugly daughter in the royal bed is encoded as a deception the king's judgment exposes when the queen returns as a duck.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
