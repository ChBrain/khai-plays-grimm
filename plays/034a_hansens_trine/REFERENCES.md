# Hans's Trine: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Hansens Trine_ (KHM 34a) as a system of extreme indolence, identity confusion, and tragic-comic self-exile. The play models Trine's complete laziness, her husband Hans's retaliatory act of cutting her skirt while she sleeps, her resulting identity crisis ("Am I me?"), her inquiry at her own house window, the household's confirmation that "Trine is inside sleeping", and her final self-exile.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812), KHM 34 (1st edition, 1812).

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 34a | The canonical first-edition tale of Hans and his lazy wife Trine. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The psychology of severe dissociation**: Trine's inability to recognize herself due to a shortened skirt is modeled as a humorous fairytale device rather than clinical dissociation.
- **The geographical destination of Trine's exile**: her journey outside the village is modeled as a simple exit to the wilderness, ignoring where she went.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
