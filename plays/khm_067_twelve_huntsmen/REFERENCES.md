# The Twelve Huntsmen: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die zwölf Jäger_ (KHM 067) as a model of hidden identity, court intrigue, and trial resilience. The production represents the twelve huntsmen not as a group of separate individuals, but as a collective camouflage unit (eleven maidens mirroring the fiancée) that neutralizes the king's detection mechanisms. The talking lion acts as a systemic truth-detector, while the tests (peas, spinning wheels) represent institutional filters designed to trigger gender-conforming behavior.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 067, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                        |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 067 | The canonical tale of the twelve huntsmen and the wise lion. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Dying King's Dynasty Politics**: the father's deathbed vow is treated strictly as an initial condition (the constraint that binds the young king's choice).
- **Animal Speech Origin**: the lion's ability to speak is accepted as a local court property (a diagnostic advisor instrument) rather than a magical transformation.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
