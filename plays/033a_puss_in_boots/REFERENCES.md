# Puss in Boots: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der gestiefelte Kater_ (KHM 33a) as a system of trickery, posturing, and magical conquest. The play models the youngest son's inheritance of a cat, the cat's demand for boots and a bag, his hunting gifts to the king, the river bathing deception, the peasants' forced declarations, the oversmarting of the sorcerer, the takeover of the castle, and the marriage to the princess.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812), KHM 33 (1st edition, 1812).

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 33a | The canonical first-edition tale of the miller's son and the cat. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The anatomy of a talking cat**: the biological possibility of a cat walking on two legs and talking is ignored; it is modeled as a given theatrical frame.
- **The physical mechanics of shape-shifting**: the wizard's shape-shifting is treated as instant state changes without physical explanation.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
