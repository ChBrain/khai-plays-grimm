# The Singing Bone: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der singende Knochen_ (KHM 028) as a single scheme of theft that holds for years and is then undone. The production models the tale not as a tragic accident but as one plan: the elder brother murders the younger who slew the boar, buries the body under the bridge, seizes the kill, and marries the princess on the stolen glory. The spear, the boar, the bone, and the carved mouthpiece are load-bearing instruments in that fraud, and the outcome turns on the one witness a murderer cannot silence, the buried bone itself.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 028, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                                 |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_, KHM 028 | The canonical tale of fratricide, stolen glory, and the singing bone. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Clean villainy**: the elder brother's win is not effortless cunning but heimtueck. Every step is delegated to [der gestohlene Ruhm](plan_stolen-glory.md), the scheme that trades on a silent corpse.
- **Supernatural rescue**: the murder's exposure is not random magic but the deliberate failure of the plan, the bone that surfaces and is carved into a horn.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
