# The Youth Who Went Forth to Learn What Fear Was: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Maerchen von einem, der auszog, das Fuerchten zu lernen_ (KHM 004) as a single problem that resists every supernatural solution and yields only to a bodily one. The production models the tale not as a parade of horrors but as a failed pedagogy: gallows, haunted castle, cats, ghosts, and giant all fail to teach the missing shudder, and the cure arrives from the most ordinary of agents, a wife with a bucket. The minnow bucket, the cold water, and the sleeping body are the load-bearing instruments of the one plan that works.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 004, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                                  |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_, KHM 004 | The canonical tale of the fearless youth and the bucket of cold water. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The shudder as moral lesson**: the youth's missing fear is modeled as a bodily deficit cured by a bodily shock, not as a character flaw corrected by wisdom.
- **Passive cure**: the youth's learning of fear is not an accident of the night. It is the work of [die Gruendlingskur im Schlaf](plan_minnow-cure.md), the princess's deliberate scheme.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
