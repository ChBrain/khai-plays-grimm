# The Three Feathers: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die drei Federn_ (KHM 063) as a systemic trial of the deep versus the shallow: an old king determining his succession through three impossible requests, resolved not by the cleverness of the older brothers but by the Dummling's obedience to the underworld. The production models the tale not as a series of random favors but as a structured covenant: the Dummling descends to the dark, respects the toad, and receives exact ritual tools (the carpet, the ring, the coach) that answer each royal command. The final test—jumping through the hoop—functions as the decisive physical checkmate against the coarse, surface-level strength of the brothers.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 063, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Arbitrary Magic**: the toad's gifts are not random. They are the execution of [die Erlangung des Reiches](plan_three-feathers.md), where descending to the underworld is the necessary condition to obtain the tokens.
- **Surface Cleverness**: the older brothers' intelligence is modeled as purely superficial, leading them to bring cheap, low-effort items because they rely on their social status, which fails the king's absolute criteria.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
