# The King of the Golden Mountain: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der König vom goldenen Berge_ (KHM 092) as a dark magical tale of a rash promise, faithful endurance, betrayal, and reconquest. The production tracks how a ruined merchant's blind bargain sells his own son, how the son's silent endurance wins him a kingdom, how a single thoughtless word and a faithless wife cost him that kingdom, and how three trickster-won wonders let him take it back by force.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 092, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                       |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 092 | The canonical tale of the sold son, the enchanted princess, and the giants. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of enchantment**: the snake-curse, the water of life, and the wishing-ring are treated as given state transitions, not as a magic system with rules or costs.
- **Geography of the journey**: the distances between town, castle, golden mountain, and the giants' hill are abstracted; the seven-league boots collapse travel into a single move.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
