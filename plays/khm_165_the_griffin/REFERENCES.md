# The Griffin: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Vogel Greif_ (KHM 165) as an illustration of how humble courtesy and willing service triumph over pride and greed. The production models the contrast between the despised youngest son, whose honest civility earns hidden help at every turn, and the arrogant brothers and word-breaking king, whose hubris and avarice turn against them until the simpleton wins the crown.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 165, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                          |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 165 | The canonical tale of Stupid Hans, the tasks, and the Griffin. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanism of the curse and cure**: the iron man's transformation of the apples into frogs' legs and hogs' bristles is treated as a moral switch keyed to courtesy, not as a magical chemistry to be simulated.
- **Physics of the land-boat and the whistle**: the boat that runs faster on land than water and the whistle that recalls the hares are modeled as state changes granted by the helper, not as engineering or acoustics.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
