# Fair Katrinelje and Pif-Paf-Poltrie: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die schöne Katrinelje und Pif Paf Poltrie_ (KHM 131) as a cumulative sequence of consents and matching transactions. The production models the tale not as a conventional romance but as a strict mechanical progression of conditional permissions, followed by a material matching of a bride's assets against a groom's craft. The suitor's search for approval from the five guardians, the counting of the sparse dowry, and the recognition of the broom-maker's trade are the structural pillars. The philological detail of _drittehalb Groschen_ (representing exactly two and a half groschen) is explicitly mapped.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 131, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 131 | The canonical nonsense/cumulative tale of wooing and dowry counting. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Romantic sentimentality**: the courtship is strictly a checklist of consents. The characters express no emotional warmth, only rule-based agreement.
- **Physical layout of the farm**: the transitions between the chamber, stable, and garden are modeled as spatial transitions between places, not physical travel.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
