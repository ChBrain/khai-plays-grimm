# Eve's Various Children: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die ungleichen Kinder Evas_ (KHM 180) as an etiological tale of the estates. The production traces how, after the expulsion from Paradise, Eve presents only her fair children to the visiting Lord and hides the plain ones, how the Lord blesses the fair to high estates and the plain to humble trades, and how he answers Eve's protest with the parable of the one body and its many limbs — modeling the diverse ranks and crafts as the necessary, mutually sustaining members of a single whole.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 180, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857. The German declared title of the tale is _Die ungleichen Kinder Evas_.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Theology of creation and the Fall**: the expulsion from Paradise and the Lord's omniscience are treated as plot premises, not as doctrine; the blessing is staged as a state-assigning lever, not explained.
- **Enumeration of every estate**: the fair and plain children are modeled as two collective personas; the lists of ranks and trades are named as the source gives them, not multiplied into separate elements.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
