# Von dem Mäuschen, Vögelchen und der Bratwurst: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Von dem Mäuschen, Vögelchen und der Bratwurst_ (KHM 023) as a domestic tragedy of role division: three companions living in harmony until they swap tasks, leading to the mouse drowning in the pot, the sausage being eaten by a dog, and the bird falling into the well.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 023, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 023 | The canonical tale of the three housemates who swapped their chores. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Inverted Domestic Order**: the tragic end is modeled as the logical consequence of abandoning the established, functional division of labor.
- **Absurd Fatalities**: the bizarre deaths are treated as physical inevitabilities when objects and animals attempt tasks outside their physical nature.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
