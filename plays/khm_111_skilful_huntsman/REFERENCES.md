# The Skilful Huntsman: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der gelernte Jäger_ (KHM 111) as an illustration of proven achievement against loud false claim. The production models how the huntsman's quietly secured tokens: the giants' tongues, the princess's slipper and scraps of cloth: overturn the one-eyed captain's empty boast, so that the true deed, not the claim, wins the daughter and the kingdom.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 111, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                            |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 111 | The canonical tale of the locksmith's son turned crack marksman. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Ballistics of the airgun**: the never-missing _Windbüchse_ is treated as a fixed guarantee of a hit, ignoring trajectory, range, or reload mechanics.
- **Topography of the lake**: the water around the tower is a simple boundary the huntsman crosses, not a modeled body with depth or current.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
