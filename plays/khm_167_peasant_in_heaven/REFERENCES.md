# The Peasant in Heaven: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das Bürle im Himmel_ (KHM 167) as a gentle satire on how worth is measured by scarcity rather than virtue. The production models the contrast between the poor peasant, whose entry into heaven is so common it needs no fanfare, and the rich lord, whose rare arrival once in a hundred years earns music and song, and lets Saint Peter resolve the apparent injustice with a mild explanation.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 167, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 167 | The canonical tale "Das Bürle im Himmel" of the peasant and lord. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Theology of salvation**: who is admitted to heaven is taken as given; the production models only the manner of welcome, not the judgment of souls.
- **Music and acoustics**: the rejoicing is treated as a simple presence-or-absence signal of celebration, not as scored or performed sound.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
