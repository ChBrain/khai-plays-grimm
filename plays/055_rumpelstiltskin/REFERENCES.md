# Rumpelstiltskin: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Rumpelstilzchen_ (KHM 055) as a collision of two schemes over a child: extortion against discovery. The production models the tale not as a riddle of luck but as a strict contest of plans, the little man's design to spin straw into gold, drive up the price, and seize the firstborn behind the shield of his hidden name, set against the queen's design to send a messenger and bring that name back within the three granted days. The straw, the gold, and above all the secret name are the load-bearing instruments in that contest, and the outcome is decided by whether the name stays hidden or is heard at the forest fire.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 055, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                                                   |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_, KHM 055 | The canonical tale of the straw spun to gold, the promised child, and the guessed name. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Magical luck**: the queen's survival is not chance. It is the work of [die Namensjagd der Koenigin](plan_name-quest.md), a deliberate plan whose messenger overhears and brings home the one word that breaks the pact.
- **The little man as mere imp**: his menace is given its own agency as [der Handel ums Kind](plan_name-bargain.md), an extortion-scheme that wins the bargains and then collapses when the name is guessed.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
