# Poverty and Humility Lead to Heaven: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Armut und Demut führen zum Himmel_ (KHM 204) as a children's legend of voluntary renunciation and the hidden sanctity of the humble. The production models a king's son who longs for heaven, is instructed by a poor gray-haired old man to embrace poverty and wander humbly for seven years, trades his fine coat for rags, begs through the world, returns home unrecognized, is sheltered under the stairs by his compassionate mother, and dies a holy death with a rose and a lily in his hands.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 204, a children's legend in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The afterlife itself**: the production halts at the threshold of heaven and refuses to depict what lies beyond the holy death.
- **The written history of his life**: treated as a plain narrative token whose existence is shown but whose full text is not invented.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
