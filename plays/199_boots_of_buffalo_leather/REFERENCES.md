# The Boots of Buffalo-Leather: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Stiefel von Büffelleder_ (KHM 199) as an illustration of fearlessness as a practical virtue. The production models how a discharged, penniless soldier turns overwhelming odds into advantage through boldness alone, binding a band of twelve robbers with nothing but a bottle of wine and a commanding word, and is rewarded for life by the king who travelled at his side in disguise.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 199, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 199 | The canonical tale of the fearless soldier in buffalo-leather boots. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanism of the spell**: the paralysis of the robbers is treated as a single state transition triggered by the soldier's command, not as a worked magical or chemical procedure.
- **Military logistics**: the king's troops binding and carting off the robbers are modeled as an off-stage resolution rather than a simulated march or muster.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
