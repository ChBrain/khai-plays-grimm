# Fitcher's Bird: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Fitchers Vogel_ (KHM 046) as a collision of two schemes inside the murderer's house: a test-trap against a counter-test of cunning. The production models the tale not as a horror of dismemberment but as a strict contest of plans, the sorcerer's design to abduct, test, and butcher each bride set against the youngest sister's design to read the test, revive the dead, and walk the trap back onto its maker. The basket, the egg, the bloody chamber, and the feather-disguise are load-bearing instruments in that contest, and the outcome is decided not by power but by who out-schemes whom.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 046, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                                |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_, KHM 046 | The canonical tale of the wizard, the test-egg, and the bird-escape. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Sanitized murder**: the bloody chamber and the dismemberment are the cold premise that sets both plans in motion, not softened away.
- **Accidental escape**: the youngest sister's survival is not luck. It is the work of [die List der juengsten Schwester](plan_bird-disguise.md), a deliberate plan whose steps each succeed in sequence.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
