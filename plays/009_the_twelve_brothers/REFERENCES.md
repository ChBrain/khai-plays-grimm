# The Twelve Brothers: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die zwoelf Brueder_ (KHM 009) as a collision of two designs by the powerful, each aimed at a sibling and each undone by loyalty. The production models the tale not as a chain of misfortunes but as a strict contest of schemes: the father-king's plan to kill his twelve sons the moment a daughter is born, set against the king-mother's plan to burn the silent sister as a witch. The coffins, the red flag, the seven-year silence, and the pyre are the load-bearing instruments of the two plans, and both backfire because each forgets the bond it cannot break.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 009, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Royal whim as fate**: the father-king's order to kill his sons is modeled as a deliberate murderous plan with a signal and a set of coffins, not as an arbitrary plot device.
- **Accidental rescue**: the brothers' escape and the sister's deliverance are not luck. They are the failures of [die Saerge und das rote Zeichen](plan_coffins-and-flag.md) and [die Hexenklage am Scheiterhaufen](plan_witch-pyre.md), each defeated by loyalty in the nick of time.
- **The witch accusation as truth**: the silent sister's stillness is encoded as enforced silence misread as witchcraft, never as actual guilt.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
