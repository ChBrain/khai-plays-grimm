# Sweetheart Roland: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der liebste Roland_ (KHM 056) as a collision of two schemes in the dark: murder against flight. The production models the tale not as a string of marvels but as a strict contest of plans, the witch's design to kill the hated stepdaughter in her bed with the axe, set against the girl's design to swap the beds, steal the wand, leave the blood-drops to answer for her, transform into lake and duck and flower, and sing her forgetful Roland back. The axe, the wand, the blood-drops, and the red flower are the load-bearing instruments in that contest, and the outcome is decided by who reads the darkness first.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 056, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                                                  |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_, KHM 056 | The canonical tale of the swapped beds, the magic flight, and the song of recognition. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Accidental escape**: the girl's survival is not luck. It is the work of [die Flucht des Maedchens](plan_girls-flight.md), a deliberate plan of swapped beds, stolen wand, blood-drop decoys, transformations, and song.
- **The witch as mere monster**: her malice is given its own agency as [der Mord im Bett](plan_night-murder.md), a murder-scheme that misfires in the dark onto her own daughter.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
