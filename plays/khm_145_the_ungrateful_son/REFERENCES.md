# The Ungrateful Son: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der undankbare Sohn_ (KHM 145) as a terse cautionary tale on filial ingratitude. The production models how a son's greedy concealment of food from his aged father converts, in a single beat, into a visible and inseparable punishment: the roast chicken becomes a toad that fastens to his face, condemning him to feed it daily and wander the world without rest.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 145, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                               |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 145 | The brief moral tale _Der undankbare Sohn_ of the chicken and toad. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanism of the transformation**: the chicken's change into a toad is treated as an instantaneous moral verdict, not as any biological or magical process to be simulated.
- **Anatomy of the toad**: the creature is modeled solely as an inseparable burden on the face, ignoring its physiology beyond its venomous look and daily feeding.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
