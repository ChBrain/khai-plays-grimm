# The Gold-Children: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Goldkinder_ (KHM 085) as a two-strand study of kept silence, transformation, and the simple life-token that binds two brothers. The production models how a withheld secret buys and unbuys wealth, how the cut-up golden fish becomes living gold (children, foals, and lilies), and how a single drooping lily turns the stay-at-home brother into the rescuer of the petrified wanderer.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 085, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                         |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 085 | The canonical tale of the golden fish, the two gold-children, and the lilies. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Biology of the golden bodies**: the gold of children, foals, and lilies is treated as a token of fate and a life-indicator, not as a material with chemical or metallurgical properties.
- **Mechanics of the cupboard and the petrification**: the self-filling cupboard and the witch's stone-spell are modeled as state transitions tied to the silence pact and the threat to the dog, not as physical processes.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
