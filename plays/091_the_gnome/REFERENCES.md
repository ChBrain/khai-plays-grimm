# The Gnome: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Dat Erdmänneken_ (KHM 091) as a rescue tale of plain courage tested against cunning and betrayal. The production models how the despised youngest huntsman, "Stupid Hans", passes the gnome's trial, descends deepest to slay the many-headed dragons, is left for dead by his treacherous companions, and is restored to the truth so that loyalty is rewarded and treachery hanged.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 091, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                      |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 091 | The Low German tale of the gnome, the dragons, and the rescued princesses. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Dragon anatomy**: the nine, five, and four heads are treated as a count of trials to overcome, not a biological or combat simulation.
- **Mechanics of the basket and bell**: the rope, basket, and signalling bell are modeled as a single fragile link between depth and surface, abstracting away pulleys and weights.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
