# Jorinda and Joringel: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Jorinde und Joringel_ (KHM 069) as a representation of boundary zones, petrification constraints, and redemptive retrieval. The production models the 100-pace radius around the castle as a physical constraint zone that triggers state changes (men to stone, women to nightingales). The resolution relies on a biological key (the red flower with the dew-pearl) which neutralizes the witch's control interface upon contact.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 069, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                           |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 069 | The canonical tale of the nightingale bride and the red flower. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Witch Shape-shifting Mechanics**: the witch's transformation into a cat or owl by day is treated as an environmental boundary state (her search parameters) rather than active character logic.
- **Dream Origin**: Joringel's prophetic dream of the flower is accepted as a narrative direction vector (the cue that triggers his search).
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
