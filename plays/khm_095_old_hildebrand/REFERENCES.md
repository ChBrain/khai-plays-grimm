# Old Hildebrand: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der alte Hildebrand_ (KHM 095) as a comic trick-tale about a pious schemer undone by a plain witness. The production models the contrast between the Parson's elaborate, sanctified deception (a feigned illness and an invented pilgrimage) and the Gossip's blunt, low-tech reversal (an egg-basket and a telltale verse) that delivers the cudgelling.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 095, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                               |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 095 | The canonical comic tale of the parson's pilgrimage and the basket. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Theology of the pilgrimage**: the Göckerli hill and its healing laurel are treated as a pure narrative lure, ignoring any real doctrine, geography, or botany.
- **Music of the feast**: the parson's fiddle and the singing are modeled as a state of merry-making, not as scored melody or lyric reconstruction.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
