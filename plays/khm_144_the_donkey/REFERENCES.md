# The Donkey: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das Eselein_ (KHM 144) as an illustration of worth hidden beneath a despised form and won back through cultivated art. The production models how an enchanted prince, born in the shape of a donkey, overcomes shame and mockery through his mastery of the lute and noble bearing, until the burning of his cast-off skin secures his true human shape and a full inheritance.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 144, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                            |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 144 | The canonical animal-bridegroom tale of the lute-playing donkey. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of the enchantment**: the curse that gives the prince a donkey's shape is treated as a given condition, not explained by spell, cause, or caster.
- **Musical theory of the lute**: the lute is modeled as a single load-bearing instrument of art and persuasion, ignoring notation, technique, or sound.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
