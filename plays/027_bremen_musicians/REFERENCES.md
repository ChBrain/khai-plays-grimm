# The Town Musicians of Bremen: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Bremer Stadtmusikanten_ (KHM 027) as a single, sustained scheme of noise over force. The production models the tale not as a lucky scare but as one plan: four cast-off animals, too old to be of use and marked for death, stack one atop another at a lit robbers' house and burst out in a single fright-concert of braying, barking, miaowing, and crowing to drive the armed band off and seize the house as a home. The window, the hearth, the claws, and the club are load-bearing instruments in that conquest, and the outcome turns on whom the robbers imagine in the dark.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 027, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Strength as victory**: the animals never win by tooth or claw alone. The house is taken by [das Schreckkonzert](plan_fright-concert.md), the scheme that makes a shared din pass for a monster.
- **Luck**: the robbers' flight is not chance but the deliberate steps of the plan, the pile-up, the crash, and the second-night ambush.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
