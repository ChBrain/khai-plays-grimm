# The Drummer: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Trommler_ (KHM 193) as a long quest tale of loyalty tested against enchantment, superhuman obstacles, and even the hero's own forgetting. The production models how a young drummer, bound by a vow, outwits a giant and two quarreling men, climbs the glass mountain by a wishing-saddle, survives the witch's three impossible tasks through the captive princess's secret wishing-ring, and finally wins her back after a kiss erases his memory.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 193, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857. The German title is "Der Trommler".

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Physics of the wishing devices**: the wishing-saddle and wishing-ring are treated as instantaneous state transitions to a desired place or completed task, ignoring any mechanism behind the magic.
- **Anatomy of the giants**: the giants are modeled only as fearful obstacles turned into transport, not as a biological or ecological population.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
