# The Aged Mother: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das alte Mütterchen_ (KHM 208) as a study of consolation drawn from a spared misfortune. The production models an aged widow who has outlived her husband, her children, all her relations, and her last friend, and who is granted a vision in a church full of her silent dead. Her dead aunt rises and shows her, at the altar, her two sons on the gallows and the wheel: the fate they would have come to had they lived, and from which God in mercy spared them by taking them as innocent children. Comforted, the old woman returns home, gives thanks, and dies peacefully three days later.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 208, a Children's Legend (Kinderlegende) of the collection, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The contents of the afterlife**: the production stages only what the tale shows, the silent congregation and the altar vision, and refuses to elaborate the theology beyond the aunt's spoken comfort.
- **Material detail of gallows and wheel**: the gallows and the wheel are treated as a single visioned image of the spared fate, not as instruments with mechanical or historical detail.
