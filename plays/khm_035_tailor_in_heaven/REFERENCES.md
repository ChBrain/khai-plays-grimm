# Der Schneider im Himmel: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Schneider im Himmel_ (KHM 035) as a satire on swift judgment and human pride: a tailor who enters Heaven, sits on God's throne, sees an old woman stealing, throws God's footstool at her in anger, and is cast out for his lack of patience.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 035, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                        |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 035 | The canonical tale of the tailor's swift judgment in Heaven. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Divine Seat**: sitting on God's throne grants the tailor total sight of all earthly deeds, driving him to immediate, self-righteous anger.
- **Tailor's Expulsion**: the cast-out status is the direct consequence of violating the throne's perspective with human impatience and judgment.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
