# Doctor Knowall: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Doktor Allwissend_ (KHM 098) as a comic study of reputation without substance: how a poor peasant, armed only with a sign and a picture book, is taken for an all-knowing doctor. The production models the gap between apparent knowledge and real knowledge, showing that the trickster's success is manufactured entirely by lucky coincidence and the guilty conscience of the thieves rather than by any skill of his own.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 098, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The two thalers**: the price of the wood is treated as a simple trigger of ambition, not as an accounted sum or economic system.
- **Medical practice**: the doctor's actual craft of healing is never modeled; the production deals only with the empty sign of the profession.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
