# The Nightingale and the Blindworm: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Von der Nachtigall und der Blindschleiche_ (KHM 6a, withdrawn after the first edition) as a short etiological scheme: two one-eyed housemates live as equals until a borrowed eye, kept against the promise, breaks the balance and turns a favour into a feud without end. The production models the tale not as a fable of manners but as the mechanism by which a single broken word becomes a permanent state of the world - the nightingale two-eyed and high, the blindworm sightless and lurking. The two eyes and the nightingale's eggs are the load-bearing instruments of that design.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, first edition (1812), tale no. 6; withdrawn from later editions and absent from the 1857 edition of last hand. Recorded among the tales "aus der ersten Auflage."

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **No reconciliation**: the tale ends in duration, not in peace; the feud is verewigt, not resolved, and the production keeps that unresolved close rather than softening it into a moral.
- **No invented third party**: there is no judge, no mediator, no second schemer. The whole design belongs to the two animals; the nightingale takes, the blindworm avenges.
