# Läuschen und Flöhchen: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Läuschen und Flöhchen_ (KHM 030) as a cumulative tragedy of contagion and grief: a louse burns herself, prompting the flea to weep, the door to creak, the broom to sweep, the cart to run, the ash-heap to burn, the tree to shake, the girl to break her jug, and the spring to flood the entire world.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 030, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                        |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 030 | The canonical cumulative tale of the grieving insects and the flooded world. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Cumulative Chain**: the narrative is modeled as a chain reaction where each element's grief triggers the next, culminating in systemic collapse.
- **Absurd Escalation**: the disproportionate response of the environment to the louse's accident is treated as an inevitable domino effect.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
