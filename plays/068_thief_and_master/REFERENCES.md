# The Thief and His Master: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _De Gaudeif un sien Meester_ (KHM 068) as a representation of shapeshifting dynamics, resource transaction, and authority subversion. The production models the magic not as an arbitrary power but as a physical transformation chain (pupil to dog, horse, fish, sparrow, cock, fox). The final duel is modeled as a state-machine chase where each form attempts to find the predatory counter-form to the opponent's current shape.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 068, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                        |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 068 | The canonical tale of the thief and the transformation duel. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Divine Intervention Mismatch**: the father's misunderstanding of the church service is treated strictly as a comic narrative trigger (the comic catalyst of the apprenticeship).
- **Infinite Magic Forms**: the shapeshifting is restricted strictly to the canonical animal forms listed in the text (dog, horse, fish, sparrow, cock, fox).
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
