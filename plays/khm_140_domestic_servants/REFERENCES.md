# Domestic Servants: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das Hausgesinde_ (KHM 140) as a pure cumulative nonsense rhyme: a call-and-response in which one speaker questions and the other answers, heaping up a household of named possessions — a man, a child, a cradle, a drudge - into an ever-growing refrain that ends with both speakers going together to Walpe. The production models meaning that arises not from sense but from rhyme, repetition, and shared movement.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 140, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                            |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 140 | The nonsense rhyme _Das Hausgesinde_ and its cumulative refrain. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Semantic content of the names**: Cham, Wild, Hippodadle and "From-thy-work-do-not-budge" are treated as pure sound, never given character or biography beyond the rhyme.
- **Geography of Walpe**: the destination is a fixed rhyme point, never a described or entered place.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
