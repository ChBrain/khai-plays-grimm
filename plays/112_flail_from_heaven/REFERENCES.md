# The Flail from Heaven: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Dreschflegel vom Himmel_ (KHM 112) as an illustration of the tall tale that secures itself against doubt by bringing back a tangible token. The production models how a boastful countryman, having sold his impossibly horned oxen, climbed a sky-high turnip tree, watched angels thresh oats, fell into a deep hole, and kept the flail as the one object that makes his story irrefutable.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 112, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                               |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Botanical growth of the tree**: the turnip seed's growth into a sky-high tree is treated as an instantaneous narrative leap, ignoring any biology of germination or wood.
- **Theology of heaven**: the threshing angels are staged only as the wonder the countryman witnesses, with no doctrine of the afterlife modeled.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
