# Thumbling: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Daumesdick_ (KHM 037) as a single, sustained scheme of cunning over size. The production models the tale not as a string of mishaps befalling a tiny boy but as one plan: Thumbling turns his smallness into guile, drives the cart from inside the horse's ear, lets himself be sold and slips away, wrecks the thieves' robbery on purpose by shouting, and steers the swallowing cow and wolf home to be cut open. The horse's ear, the hay, the money, the cow, and the wolf are load-bearing instruments in that survival, and the outcome turns on every giant overrating what he thinks he holds.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 037, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Helplessness**: smallness is never a weakness here. Every escape is delegated to [die List des Winzlings](plan_tiny-cunning.md), the scheme that makes tininess a hiding place and a trap.
- **Luck**: the survival of the cow's and wolf's bellies is not chance but the deliberate steps of the plan, the hay, the shout, and the steering voice.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
