# The Pack of Ragamuffins: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das Lumpengesindel_ (KHM 010) as a single roguish scheme: a cock and hen bribe their way into an inn with an egg and shells, feast through the night at the host's cost, and slip away before dawn after planting a pin and needle so the host, not they, is punished. The production models the tale not as a merry wander but as one trick run from end to end, the swindle of a trusting host. The nutshell carriage, the egg, the pin, and the needle are the load-bearing instruments of that design, and the close is decided not by justice restored but by the rogues escaping clean.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 010, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 010 | The canonical tale of the nutshell ride, the inn, and the pranks. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Restored order**: the host's vow never to lodge travelers again is the sour close of the tale; the rogues go unpunished, and this is not softened into comeuppance.
- **A counter-scheme**: there is no Gegenplan. The host suspects nothing and yields in good faith; the whole motion belongs to [der Betrug am Wirtshaus](plan_inn-swindle.md), the cock and hen's plan alone.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
