# The Six Servants: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die sechs Diener_ (KHM 134) as a system of cooperation: a prince utilizing the unique, supernatural capabilities of six hired servants to overcome the lethal challenges set by a wicked sorceress queen, leading to the humbling of the proud princess. The production models the ATU 513A classification. The recruitment, the tasks (the Red Sea ring, the giant feast, the night watch), the fire test, the flight defense, and the swineherd trick form the structural framework.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 134, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                         |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 134 | The canonical tale of the prince and his six extraordinarily gifted servants. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Servants' physiology**: the physiological origin and mechanics of the servants' powers are not modeled, only their inputs and outputs.
- **Geography of the 300 leagues**: the rapid travel is modeled as a transition between places, ignoring the physical geometry of the terrain.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
