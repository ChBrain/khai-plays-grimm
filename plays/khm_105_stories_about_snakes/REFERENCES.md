# Stories About Snakes: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Märchen von der Unke_ (KHM 105) as a triptych of three short snake-fragments. The production models the fragile hospitality between humans and house-snakes (Unken) and the ways it shatters: the first fragment through a mother's fearful violence, the second through an orphan's theft of the snake's golden crown, and the third through an open, unresolved cry that finds no answer.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 105, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                       |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 105 | The three short tales of the Unke (_Märchen von der Unke_). |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Connective plot between fragments**: the three fragments are treated as discrete tableaux; no overarching narrative is invented to bind them.
- **Biology of the Unke**: the snake is modeled as a trusting guest and gift-giver, ignoring zoological detail of the toad or snake.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
