# Lazy Harry: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der faule Heinz_ (KHM 164) as a comic study of idleness, in which the avoidance of every small daily chore steadily dissolves the only real gain. The production models how Harry and Trina trade away each duty in turn, goats for bees and honey for a dreamed-of goose, until a careless swing of the hazel-stick shatters the honey-jar and leaves them with nothing but rest.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 164, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                              |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 164 | The canonical tale of lazy Harry, fat Trina, and the broken honey. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The unborn child and ungotten goose**: the quarrel's child and the goose with its gosling are treated as objects of idle talk only, never staged as real beings.
- **The economics of bartering**: the neighbour's trade is modeled as a single state transition of goods for goods, not a simulation of market value or husbandry.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
