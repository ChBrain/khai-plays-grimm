# Gambling Hansel: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Spielhansl_ (KHM 082) as a picaresque illustration of an addiction that no grace can cure. The production models a compulsive gambler who turns every gift, even the Lord's three wishes, into fresh stakes, defeating Death and Hell with enchanted tools yet never his own vice, until his soul shatters into the gambling vagabonds of today.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 082, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                           |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 082 | The canonical tale of Der Spielhansl, the unredeemable gambler. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Theology of the afterlife**: Heaven, Purgatory, and Hell are treated as narrative destinations that admit or reject the soul, not as doctrinal claims about salvation.
- **Mechanics of the games**: the cards and dice are modeled as deterministic always-win tokens, ignoring the actual rules of any card or dice game.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
