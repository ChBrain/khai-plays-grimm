# Foundling-Bird: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Fundevogel_ (KHM 051) as a collision of two schemes between a murderous cook and two inseparable children: a boiling against a flight by transformation. The production models the tale not as a chase fable but as a strict contest of plans, the cook's design to seize and boil Foundling-Bird set against the children's design to flee together and shed each wave of pursuers by metamorphosis until the cook herself drowns. The water-buckets, the nest, and the pond-plain are load-bearing instruments in that contest, and the outcome is decided not by speed but by whose plan turns the pursuit back on its maker.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 051, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                                   |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_, KHM 051 | The canonical tale of the foundling, the cook, and the transformations. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Sanitized malice**: the cook's plan to boil the foundling in the kettle is the cold premise that sets both plans in motion, not softened away.
- **Accidental escape**: the children's survival is not luck. It is the work of [die Flucht der Kinder](plan_children-flight.md), a deliberate plan whose transformations each succeed in sequence.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
