# Snow-White and Rose-Red: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Schneeweißchen und Rosenrot_ (KHM 161) as an illustration of selfless kindness that endures ingratitude and is rewarded at last. The production models the contrast between the gentle, homebound Snow-White and the lively, roaming Rose-Red, the loyal bond formed by sheltering the enchanted bear, the threefold rescue of the thankless dwarf, and the breaking of the curse that frees a king's son.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 161, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                  |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 161 | The canonical tale of the two sisters, the bear, and the wicked dwarf. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Botanical detail of the rose-trees**: the white and red roses are treated as a symbolic naming and a steady emblem of the sisters, ignoring the biology of the plants.
- **The dwarf's enchantment mechanics**: the curse binding the prince into the bearskin is modeled as a single state that ends with the dwarf's death, not as a detailed magical system.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
