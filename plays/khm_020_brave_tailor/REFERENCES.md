# The Brave Little Tailor: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das tapfere Schneiderlein_ (KHM 020) as a single, sustained scheme of cunning over strength. The production models the tale not as a string of lucky feats but as one plan: a tiny man parlays a trivial deed (seven flies killed at one blow) into a monstrous reputation, then rides that borrowed glory through giants, court, and deadly trials up to half the kingdom. The embroidered belt, the cheese, the bird, the boar-trap, and the night-time boast are load-bearing instruments in that climb, and the outcome turns on who reads the boast wrong.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 020, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_, KHM 020 | The canonical tale of the boast, the giants, and the won kingdom. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Strength as victory**: the tailor never wins by force. Every feat is delegated to [Sieben auf einen Streich](plan_seven-at-one-blow.md), the scheme that turns each rival's own strength against him.
- **Luck**: the survival of the giants' bed and the king's traps is not chance but the deliberate steps of the plan.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
