# The Old Man and His Grandson: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der alte Großvater und der Enkel_ (KHM 078) as a moral fable on filial duty and the reciprocity of care across generations. The production models how a child's artless imitation becomes a mirror that holds the parents' own conduct before them, turning contempt for the feeble grandfather into shame and restoring him to the family table.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 078, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                           |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Material detail of the bowls**: the earthenware and wooden bowls are treated as simple props for scarce, scornful fare, ignoring the craft or composition of the vessels.
- **Domestic geography**: the home is modeled as the table set against the corner behind the stove, rather than a full floor plan of the dwelling.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
