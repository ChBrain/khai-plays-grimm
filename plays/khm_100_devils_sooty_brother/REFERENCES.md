# The Devil's Sooty Brother: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Des Teufels rußiger Bruder_ (KHM 100) as an illustration of how faithful endurance in the lowest and dirtiest service is rewarded over time. The production models a disbanded soldier who serves the Devil for seven years under harsh conditions, earns wages that look worthless but turn to gold, and rises through theft and recovery to win a kingdom.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 100, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                   |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 100 | The canonical soldier's-luck tale of the Devil's sooty serving-brother. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Theology of Hell**: Hell is treated as a workplace of fires and kettles with a master and conditions of service, not as a doctrinal afterlife.
- **Transmutation mechanics**: the sweepings becoming gold above ground is modeled as a state change at the boundary of Hell, not as a chemical or magical process.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
