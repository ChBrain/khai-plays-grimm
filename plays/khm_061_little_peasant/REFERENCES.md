# The Little Peasant: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das Buerle_ (KHM 061) as a single deliberate scheme: the snare of greed. The production models the tale not as loose trickster comedy but as one chain of cons turning the villagers' avarice to ruin. The poorest man in the village, owning no cow, passes off the wooden calf's "loss," sells the cowhide as a soothsayer's, tricks the herdsman into trading places in the cask, and lures the greedy rich peasants to drown chasing imagined sheep beneath the river. The wooden calf, the cowhide, the thalers, and the barrel are the load-bearing instruments of that scheme, which succeeds at every step.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 061, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Sanitized cunning**: the lies, the swindled thalers, and the drowning of the greedy neighbors are modeled as the cold logic of the plan, not softened into harmless mischief.
- **Accidental fortune**: the peasant's wealth is not luck. It is the worked-through result of [die Falle der Gier](plan_greed-snare.md), a single plan whose steps land in sequence.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
