# The Crystal Ball: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Kristallkugel_ (KHM 197) as an illustration of fearless persistence and cooperation breaking a fixed enchantment. The production models how the youngest son escapes his mother's curse, wins the wishing-cap from the quarreling giants, and recovers the crystal ball hidden inside the fiery egg, so that with the help of his transformed brothers the eagle and the whale he breaks the enchanter's power and restores the true forms hidden behind the disfiguring spell.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 197, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                              |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 197 | The canonical tale of the crystal ball and the bewitched princess. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of the transformations**: the brothers' shapes as eagle and whale are treated as simple state changes bound to mountains and sea, not as biological or zoological detail.
- **Theology of the enchantments**: the mother's curse and the enchanter's spell are modeled only as bindings broken by the crystal ball, not as metaphysical forces.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
