# Die Wichtelmänner: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Wichtelmänner_ (KHM 039) as a three-part chronicle of helper spirits: a poor shoemaker assisted by nocturnal elves who leave when given clothes, a servant girl who serves as godmother for the elves, and a mother who rescues her child from a changeling left by the elves.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 039, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                               |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 039 | The canonical three-part tale of the helpful and mischievous elves. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Nocturnal Assistance**: the elves' labor is bound by secret visibility; once they are observed and rewarded with clothes, the covenant ends.
- **Temporal Distortion**: the servant girl's visit to the elf mountain is modeled as a temporal trap, where three days in the mountain equal three years on Earth.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
