# The White Bride and the Black Bride: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die weiße und die schwarze Braut_ (KHM 135) as a system of substitution and restoration. The play models the ATU 403A classification, focusing on the blessing and curses, Reginer's portrait, the journey substitution, the snake pit punishment, the duck's kitchen visitations, and the final exposure and retribution.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 135, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                           |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 135 | The canonical tale of the white bride, her brother Reginer, and the usurpation. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The nature of the Lord's disguise**: God's disguise is modeled as a simple poor man persona without theological representation.
- **Physical bridge construction**: the bridge over the river is modeled as a shown element of the road, ignoring the structural engineering.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
