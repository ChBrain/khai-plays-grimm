# The Star-Money: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Sterntaler_ (KHM 153) as an illustration of unconditional charity and trust under total dispossession. The production models how a destitute orphan who gives away her every possession, down to the last shirt, out of pure compassion and faith is repaid with an overflowing, unearned reward from heaven.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 153, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                        |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 153 | The canonical tale of the orphan girl and the falling stars. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Theology of the miracle**: the falling stars turning to coin are treated as a simple narrative reward state, not a doctrinal account of divine grace.
- **Identity of the needy**: the hungry man and the four children are modeled as successive occasions for giving, not as characters with biographies of their own.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
