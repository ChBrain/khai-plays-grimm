# The Hare's Bride: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Hasenbraut_ (KHM 066) as a representation of isolation, boundary intrusion, and escape via decoy. The production models the cabbage garden as a contested boundary between human cultivation and the animal wilderness. The hare's repeated invitation to sit on his tail acts as a hypnotic compulsion that leads the girl into isolation. The resolution relies on a physical substitute (the straw doll) which performs the role of the silent bride while the actual girl escapes back to her mother.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 066, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                        |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 066 | The canonical tale of the cabbage patch and the straw decoy. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Incestuous or Animal Marriage Realism**: the wedding of the hare and the girl is treated strictly as a surreal, social confinement rather than a literal marriage.
- **Intrusive Agency**: the crow and fox acting as clergy are modeled as static elements of the confinement environment rather than characters with independent subplots.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
