# Thumbling's Travels: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Daeumlings Wanderschaft_ (KHM 045) as a single scheme of cunning smallness. The production models the tale not as a loose string of misadventures but as the deliberate plan of Thumbling: turn his tiny size into a weapon on his journeyman travels, taunt the master from a mouse-hole, help the robbers by tossing coins out through the bars unseen, mock his captors, and ride home inside the cow and the wolf to be freed. The needle-sword, the gold coins, and the beasts' bellies are load-bearing instruments, and the outcome is survival by wit rather than strength.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 045, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Smallness as helplessness**: Thumbling's size is not a handicap to be pitied. It is the engine of [die List des Daeumlings](plan_thumbling-cunning.md), a deliberate plan that makes every crack and grate a path the large cannot take.
- **Rescue by luck**: the homecoming is not chance. It is steered, the swallowed traveler driving the greedy wolf to his parents' door so the beast can be cut open.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
