# The Twelve Apostles: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die zwölf Apostel_ (KHM 202) as a children's legend of faithful petition and bewahrung across time. The production models a poor mother who, three hundred years before the birth of Christ, prays that her twelve sons may live to see the promised Redeemer. Sent out into the world, the eldest, Peter, wanders starving through a great forest until a small radiant angel-child leads him to a crystalline cavern. One by one the twelve brothers arrive and are laid in twelve golden cradles, where they sleep for three hundred years and wake, on the night of Christ's birth, as the twelve apostles.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 202, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857, where it stands among the children's legends as "Die zwölf Apostel".

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Theology of the apostles**: the legend names the awakening as the twelve apostles, and the production keeps this as narrative closure without expanding doctrine or scripture.
- **Material detail of the cavern and cradles**: the crystalline cavern and golden cradles are treated as plain narrative tokens of bewahrung, not as objects with physical properties.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
