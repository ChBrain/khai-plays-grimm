# The True Sweethearts: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die wahre Braut_ (KHM 186) as an illustration of steadfast fidelity outlasting imposed trials, betrayal, and even the forgetting of the beloved. The production models the contrast between the maiden's unbroken constancy and patience against the stepmother's torment, the prince's broken vow, and the long descent into a cowherd's grief, until a single kiss restores the rightful union.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 186, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                  |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 186 | The canonical tale of the true bride, the three dresses, and the kiss. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of the magic**: the old woman's wonders (reading the feathers, emptying the pond, raising the castle) are treated as instantaneous miracles, not as physical or magical procedures to be simulated.
- **Court politics of the false bride**: the rival the prince almost weds is modeled only as the boundary condition of the festival, not as a developed actor with her own intrigue.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
