# Going A-Travelling: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Up Reisen gohn_ (KHM 143) as a study of context-blind instruction: a simpleton is handed a correct phrase at each place and is beaten for repeating it at the next, because a right word only helps in the right situation. The production models how mechanical repetition of a learned rule, without grasp of the occasion, turns every well-meant lesson into a fresh injury.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 143, told in Low German as _Up Reisen gohn_ and rendered in English as "Going A-Travelling".

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                   |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 143 | The cumulative comic tale of the son beaten for saying the wrong words. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The hanging itself**: the execution at the gallows is treated as a fixed backdrop demanding solemnity, not as a judicial or physical process to be simulated.
- **The knacker's craft**: the skinning of the horse is reduced to the occasion that makes a pious word sound like mockery, ignoring the trade's actual technique.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
