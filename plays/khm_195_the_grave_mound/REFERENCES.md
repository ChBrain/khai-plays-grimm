# The Grave-Mound: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Grabhügel_ (KHM 195) as an illustration of fearless cunning and faithful promise defeating greed. The production models how a repentant miser's soul, forfeit to the Devil, is saved at his grave-mound when a discharged soldier bargains the Devil into filling a bottomless boot set over a hidden pit, so that cock-crow drives the Devil off empty-handed and the gathered gold passes to the poor.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 195, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                           |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 195 | The canonical tale "Der Grabhügel" of the soldier and the boot. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Theology of the soul**: the miser's soul is modeled only as a contested stake claimed by the Devil and saved by cock-crow, not as a metaphysical doctrine of judgment.
- **Mechanics of the bottomless boot**: the soleless boot over the pit is treated as a simple unfillable vessel, ignoring the real volume of the gold or the physics of the hidden hole.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
