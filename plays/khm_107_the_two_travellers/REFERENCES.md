# The Two Travellers: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die beiden Wanderer_ (KHM 107) as an illustration of how active, uncalculated generosity prevails over cold cruelty. The production models the contrast between the merry tailor, who shares his bread and spares every creature, and the ill-tempered shoemaker, who hoards seven days' bread and buys the tailor's eyes for crumbs. The grateful animals the tailor spares become the instruments that solve the king's impossible tasks and raise him to the throne, while the slanderer's own knife-work returns to him under the gallows.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 107, declared in German as _Die beiden Wanderer_, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Medical detail of the blinding and healing**: the loss and restoration of the tailor's sight are treated as narrative state transitions, not as anatomy or physiology; the dew of the gallows is a token of grace, not a remedy.
- **Animal behaviour**: the duck's diving, the bees' wax-work, the horse's stamping, and the stork's errand are modeled as the discharge of a promised debt, not as biological capability.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
