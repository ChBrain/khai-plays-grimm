# The Fox and the Horse: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Fuchs und das Pferd_ (KHM 132) as a clash of two plans within a rural/forest setting: the peasant's expulsion plan and the fox's rescue plan. The production models the tale not as a simple moral fable but as a strategic operation of deception and physical execution. The old horse's banishment under an impossible condition (capturing a lion), the forest meeting, the luring and binding of the lion to the horse's tail, and the dragging of the captive beast back to the peasant's door constitute the structural framework. The horse's tail and the lion's cave are the key physical instruments.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 132, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                   |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 132 | The canonical tale of the old horse dragging a lion to secure its home. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Wild animal geometry**: the physical layout of the forest and the exact positioning of the lion's cave are not modeled spatially, but represented as discrete places.
- **Lion's physical combat**: the binding of the lion's legs is modeled as a successful transaction of deception, not a physical fight.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
