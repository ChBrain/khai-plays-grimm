# The Three Black Princesses: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _De drei schwatten Prinzessinnen_ (KHM 137) as a system of escape, probation, and tragic violation. The play models the ATU 401A classification, focusing on the East India siege, the father's rise to burgomaster, the gallows threat, the enchanted mountain castle, the year-long silence and non-observation trial, the home visit and near-hanging, the smock-frock revelation, the mother's boiling water advice, the violation of the sleeping princesses, the final verboten curse, and the sinking of the castle.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 137, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                         |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 137 | The canonical tale of the three black princesses, the fisherman, and his son. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The geography of East India**: the location of East India is modeled as a simple town setting, ignoring any geopolitical or historical accuracy.
- **The chemical reaction of boiling water**: the boiling water's physical effect on the princesses' skin is ignored and modeled purely as a magical state change (half-white transformation).
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
