# The Old Man Made Young Again: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das junggeglühte Männlein_ (KHM 147) as a comic etiological fable of botched imitation. The production models the contrast between the Lord's gracious renewal of the old beggar and the smith's presumptuous attempt to repeat the miracle on his mother-in-law with the same forge, bellows, and quenching-tub but without the grace that alone makes it work, ending in the origin of the race of apes.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 147, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                           |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 147 | The canonical tale of "Das junggeglühte Männlein" and the apes. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Theology of the miracle**: the Lord's power to renew is treated as a given, unexplained grace, not as a doctrine to be argued or systematized.
- **Forge metallurgy**: the heat of the coal fire and the quench in the tub are modeled as narrative transformations, not as real thermodynamics or smithing technique.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
