# The Beam: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Hahnenbalken_ (KHM 149) as an illustration of perception, illusion, and the social authority of the loudest interpreter. The production models how true sight (the four-leaved clover) can expose a deception once, yet how the same crowd that punished the deceiver will turn on the truthful seer when a confident voice reframes her clear water as a field of flax.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 149, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 149 | The canonical tale "Der Hahnenbalken" of the beam and the clover. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of the conjuring**: the enchanter's craft is treated as an opaque effect on perception, not a simulated trick of optics or sleight of hand.
- **Botany of clover and flax**: the four-leaved clover and the blue flax are modeled as carriers of true and false sight, ignoring their biological properties.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
