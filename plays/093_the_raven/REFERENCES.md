# The Raven: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Rabe_ (KHM 093) as a quest-and-disenchantment tale about fidelity, patience, and perseverance. The production models how a rescuer who fails the easy path through temptation must redeem his pledge by the long, arduous road: winning a giant with never-ending food, outwitting three quarrelling robbers for their magic tokens, and scaling the glass mountain to the golden castle of Stromberg, where an engraved ring restores recognition.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 093, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                  |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 093 | The canonical tale of the cursed princess, the raven, and the rescuer. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of enchantment**: the curse and its undoing are treated as instantaneous state changes triggered by a spoken word and a recognised token, not as a magical system to be simulated.
- **Cartography of the giants**: the brother's maps that locate the castle are taken as a given oracle of place, ignoring how such maps were drawn or kept.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
