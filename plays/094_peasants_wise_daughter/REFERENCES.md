# The Peasant's Wise Daughter: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die kluge Bauerntochter_ (KHM 094) as a study of how quiet intelligence outlasts arbitrary power. The production models the contrast between the King's capricious authority (impossible demands, wrongful judgments, wounded pride) and the Daughter's literal-minded cleverness (the net-and-ass riddle solution, the dry-land fishing counsel, the sleeping-draught stratagem) that bends each royal word back upon itself.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 094, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                               |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 094 | The canonical tale of the clever peasant girl who outwits the king. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Metallurgy of the mortar**: the golden mortar and its missing pestle are treated as a narrative lure, ignoring assaying, weight, or smithing detail.
- **Animal husbandry of the foal**: the disputed foal and the oxen are modeled as a legal token of ownership, not as livestock biology or breeding.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
