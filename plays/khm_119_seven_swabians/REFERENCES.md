# The Seven Swabians: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die sieben Schwaben_ (KHM 119) as a study of herd courage and the failure of collective perception. The production models how seven men bound to a single shared spear act as one body, so that the leader's every fright multiplies instantly down the line, turning a beetle, a hare, and a frog's croak into mortal enemies that drive all seven to their death.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 119, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                  |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 119 | The canonical noodlehead tale "Die sieben Schwaben" and the one spear. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Insect and animal behavior**: the hornet's buzz, the sleeping hare, and the croaking frog are treated as harmless triggers, ignoring any biological detail beyond the sound or sight that the Swabians misread.
- **Hydrology of the Moselle**: the river is modeled as a simple impassable boundary that drowns whoever enters, not as a simulated current or depth profile.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
