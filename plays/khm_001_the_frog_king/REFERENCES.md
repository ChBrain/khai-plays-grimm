# Der Froschkönig oder der eiserne Heinrich: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Froschkönig oder der eiserne Heinrich_ (KHM 001) as a mechanical trial of a broken promise: a princess who loses her golden ball, makes a desperate vow to a frog, and is forced by her father to keep it, leading to the breaking of the curse when she throws the frog against the wall in anger.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 001, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                     |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 001 | The canonical tale of the frog prince and faithful Henry. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Arbitrary Transformation**: the frog's transformation into a prince is not a random magic trick; it is the consequence of [the curse-breaking process](process_curse-breaking.md) triggered when the princess acts out of raw anger.
- **King's Judgment**: the father's command is absolute; the princess must keep her promise regardless of her disgust, showing the binding nature of the legal covenant.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
