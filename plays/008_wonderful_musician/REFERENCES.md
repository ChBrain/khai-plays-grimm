# The Strange Musician: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der wunderliche Spielmann_ (KHM 008) as a single scheme of cunning solitude: a lonely fiddler lures each beast of the forest close with his playing on the pretext of teaching it, then traps it and travels on alone until he finds a true human companion. The production models the tale not as a charming wander but as one player's repeated design, the fiddle as both bait and tool. The cleft oak, the bent hazel, and the noose are the load-bearing instruments of that design, and the outcome is decided not by strength but by who is lured before the trap is seen.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 008, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                  |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 008 | The canonical tale of the fiddle, the three traps, and the woodcutter. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Innocent music**: the fiddle is not idle charm but a deliberate lure; the playing and the trapping are one act, not softened into accident.
- **A counter-scheme**: there is no Gegenplan. The beasts follow the sound in good faith and feel the trap only as it closes; the whole design belongs to [die Fallen des Spielmanns](plan_fiddle-snares.md), the musician's plan alone.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
