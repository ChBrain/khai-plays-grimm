# The Twelve Idle Servants: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die zwölf faulen Knechte_ (KHM 151\*) as a cumulative catalogue of sloth: an escalating contest of laziness in which twelve servants, known only by their ordinal, sprawl on the grass at evening and each boasts of an idleness greater than the last. The production models how sloth, once it is praised without shame, outbids itself in ever bolder degrees until the master must carry the whole burden of his idle servants himself.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), the sub-numbered tale KHM 151\* "Die zwölf faulen Knechte", a companion piece to KHM 151 "Die drei Faulen", added in the definitive 7th edition of 1857 and slotted between KHM 151 and KHM 152.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Individual servants**: the twelve are treated as one collective persona ordered by their boasts, not as twelve separately characterized figures.
- **Off-stage labour**: the master's farm work, household, and travel are referenced only as the duties the servants shirk, never simulated in detail.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
