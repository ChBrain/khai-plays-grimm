# The Twelve Dancing Princesses: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die zertanzten Schuhe_ (KHM 133) as a collision of two plans within a palace/underground environment: the princesses' secret dancing plan and the soldier's discovery plan. The production models the tale not as a simple romantic adventure but as a systemic audit. The secure, locked bedchamber, the secret descent through the trapdoor, the passage through the silver and gold forests, the grand ball in the underground castle, and the collection of physical evidence (broken twigs, golden cup) form the structural framework. The invisibility cloak, the worn-out shoes, and the tokens (branches, cup) are the key physical instruments.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 133, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                        |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 133 | The canonical tale of the twelve princesses and the soldier's investigation. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Princes' identities**: the twelve underground princes are not modeled as distinct personas, but represented as part of the background environment of the grand ball.
- **Physical layout of the underground passage**: the descent is modeled as a transition between places, ignoring the physical geometry of the stairs.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
