# Ferdinand the Faithful: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Ferenand getrü und Ferenand ungetrü_ (KHM 126) as an illustration of how unwavering faithfulness, rewarded by enchanted helpers, raises the lowest servant above treachery and impossible errands to the throne. The production models the contrast between the Faithful, whose small acts of loyalty and mercy summon decisive aid, and the Unfaithful, whose envy turns every plot against himself.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 126, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                        |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 126 | The canonical tale of the faithful outrider, the white horse, and the bride. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of the magic**: the beheading and restoring, and the horse's transformation, are treated as state changes triggered by the right deed, not as anatomical or thaumaturgic simulation.
- **Geography of the lake**: the crossing to the distant princess is a topological passage gated by appeasing its inhabitants, ignoring real navigation or distance.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
