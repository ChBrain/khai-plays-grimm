# The Wolf and the Man: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Wolf und der Mensch_ (KHM 072) as a representation of arrogance, civilization tools, and defensive capability. The production models the hierarchy of power between nature (the wolf's physical strength) and human cultural technology (firearms, cold blades). The resolution demonstrates how animal boasting is corrected through painful physical chastisement, showing that humans are not to be underestimated due to their tools.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 072, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                          |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 072 | The canonical tale of the boastful wolf, the fox, and the man. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Wolf and Fox Animal Anatomy**: the characters are treated as symbolic representations of warning and boasting, rather than biologically detailed wild canines.
- **Hunter's internal thought process**: the hunter acts strictly as a mechanical defensive unit returning fire, rather than a narrative protagonist with internal dialogue.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
