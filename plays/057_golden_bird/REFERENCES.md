# The Golden Bird: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der goldene Vogel_ (KHM 057) as a collision of two schemes over the same prizes: obedient quest against envious robbery. The production models the tale not as a string of marvels but as a strict contest of plans, the youngest prince's design to win the golden bird, the golden horse, and the princess by following the fox's exact instructions, set against the elder brothers' design to rob him, throw him down the well, and claim the spoils as their own. The golden cage, the saddle, and the well are the load-bearing instruments in that contest, and the outcome is decided by who obeys the fox and who betrays.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 057, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Effortless triumph**: the prince's success is not luck. It is the work of [die Fuchsfahrt des Juengsten](plan_fox-quest.md), a deliberate plan whose steps fail each time a warning is ignored and win only when the fox is obeyed.
- **The brothers as mere foils**: their treachery is given its own agency as [der Raub der Brueder](plan_brothers-robbery.md), a robbery-scheme that succeeds for a time and then is exposed.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
