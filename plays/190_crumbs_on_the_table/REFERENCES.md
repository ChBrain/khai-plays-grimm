# The Crumbs on the Table: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Brosamen auf dem Tisch_ (KHM 190) as a brief comic anecdote on irresponsible counsel and misplaced blame. The production models how a tempter's careless advice, which conceals the cost, moves the weak to act, and how the punishment falls on them alone while the instigator only laughs.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 190, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                       |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 190 | The brief tale of the puppies, the crumbs, and the beating. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Domestic interior**: the parlour and table are treated as a simple topological placement of reachable crumbs, ignoring furniture, hearth, or household detail.
- **Canine behaviour**: the puppies' hesitation and feeding are modeled as state transitions triggered by the farmer's urging, rather than full animal cognition.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
