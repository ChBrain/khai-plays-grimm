# The Wedding of Mrs. Fox: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Hochzeit der Frau Fuechsin_ (KHM 038) as a single, sustained scheme of suspicion. The production models the tale not as a comic mix-up but as one plan: Mr. Fox feigns his own death on the stove bench to test his wife's fidelity and flush out the vain nine-tailed suitors, then rises and reclaims her. The stove bench, the shutters, the tails, the red stockings, and the wedding feast are load-bearing instruments in that test, and the outcome turns on the suitors betraying themselves to a man they think is dead.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 038, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Open inquiry**: the fox never simply asks after his wife's loyalty. The whole test is delegated to [der Scheintod des Fuchses](plan_feigned-death.md), the scheme that makes a sham funeral into a trap.
- **Coincidence**: the suitors' exposure is not chance but the deliberate steps of the plan, the maid's reports and the sudden waking.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
