# The Riddle: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das Raetsel_ (KHM 022) as a collision of two schemes at one court: an unsolvable riddle against a thieving espionage. The production models the tale not as a courtship curiosity but as a strict contest of plans, the prince's design to forge a riddle no one can answer and trap the princess's spies in the act set against the princess's design to steal the answer from the sleeping suitor over three nights. The poison, the dead horse, the raven, the twelve murderers, the cloak, and the riddle-text are load-bearing instruments in that contest, and the outcome is decided not by learning but by who out-schemes whom.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 022, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Sanitized killing**: the chain of deaths (horse, raven, twelve murderers) is modeled as the cold material of the riddle, not softened away.
- **Accidental victory**: the prince's win is not luck. It is the work of [die Raetselfalle des Prinzen](plan_riddle-snare.md), a deliberate plan whose steps succeed in sequence, with the servant's watch as its hinge.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
