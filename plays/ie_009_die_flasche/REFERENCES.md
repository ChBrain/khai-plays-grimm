# The Legend of Bottle Hill: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _The Legend of Bottle Hill_ as a study of sudden fortune and the ease of losing it. The production models how a destitute peasant, parting with his last beast on the road to market, trades want for a self-serving vessel, and how the same credulity that wins the bottle threatens to squander it.

## Origin

The Brothers Grimm's _Irische Elfenmärchen_ (1826), their German translation of Thomas Crofton Croker's _Fairy Legends and Traditions of the South of Ireland_ (1825).

| Source             | Key Work / Event                             | Scope                                                                 |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Irische Elfenmärchen_ (after Croker)        | The German rendering through which the tale enters the Grimm house.   |
| **T. C. Croker**   | _Fairy Legends_, "The Legend of Bottle Hill" | The Irish legend of the grey stranger, the cow, and the magic bottle. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of the bottle**: the vessel's setting of a laden table is treated as an immediate state transition on command, not as simulated conjuring.
- **The grey stranger**: the giver is staged only as the counterparty of the exchange at the bridge, not as a fairy whose nature or realm is modelled.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
