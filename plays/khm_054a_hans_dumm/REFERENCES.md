# Foolish Hans: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Hans Dumm_ (KHM 54a, withdrawn after the first edition) as a scheme of judgement reversed: a king lets only fair folk into the church so a fatherless child may choose its father with a lemon, but the child gives it to a small, hunchbacked youth held for a simpleton; cast out to sea in a barrel, Hans wishes food, ship, castle, and his own fair form into being; and when the king comes as an unwitting guest, the princess slips a golden cup into his baggage to hold his own rash judgement before him before she reveals herself. The production models the tale as the lesson against judging by appearance and in haste, owned by the princess. The lemon, the barrel, the wish-power, and the golden cup are the load-bearing instruments of that design.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, first edition (1812), tale no. 54; replaced from the second edition by _Der Ranzen, das Hütlein und das Hörnlein_ and absent from the 1857 edition of last hand. Recorded among the tales "aus der ersten Auflage."

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Casting-out without cruelty**: the barrel set adrift on the sea is staged as the danger and the turning point ([die Aussetzung](process_aussetzung.md), [Tonne](piece_tonne.md)), not as drawn-out suffering or death; it becomes the cradle of the rescue.
- **Lesson, not revenge**: the golden-cup ruse aims at the king's insight, not his ruin; the whole design belongs to [die Lehre der Königstochter](plan_lehre.md), and it closes in reconciliation.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
