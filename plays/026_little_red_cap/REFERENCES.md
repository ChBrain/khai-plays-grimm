# Little Red Cap: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Rotkaeppchen_ (KHM 026) as a collision of two schemes in one wood: disguised predation against vigilant rescue. The production models the tale not as a simple cautionary fable but as a strict contest of plans, the wolf's design to stall the girl with flowers, race ahead, devour the grandmother, disguise himself, and swallow the child set against the huntsman's design to cut the swallowed pair from the sleeping thief and weight him dead with stones. The cap, the bed, the scissors, and the stones are load-bearing instruments in that contest, and the outcome is decided not by strength but by who out-schemes whom.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 026, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                         |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_, KHM 026 | The canonical tale of the wolf, the disguise, and the rescue. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Sanitized devouring**: the swallowing of grandmother and child whole is modeled as the cold midpoint that both plans turn on, not softened away.
- **Accidental rescue**: the survival of the two is not luck. It is the work of [die Rettung des Jaegers](plan_huntsman-rescue.md), a deliberate plan whose steps succeed in sequence.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
