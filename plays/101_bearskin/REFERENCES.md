# Bearskin: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Bärenhäuter_ (KHM 101) as an illustration of endurance under a binding contract and the moral weighing of compassion against pride. The production models the discharged soldier's seven-year pact with the Devil, the redemptive power of his charity toward the ruined old man, the faithful patience of the youngest daughter, and the self-destruction of the two scornful sisters whose pride hands the Devil two souls in place of one.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 101, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Theology of the soul**: the wager over souls is treated as a narrative contract with fixed terms, not as a doctrinal account of damnation or salvation.
- **Mechanics of the endless money**: the green coat's pocket is modeled as an unlimited source bound to the pact, ignoring any economy or origin of the gold.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
