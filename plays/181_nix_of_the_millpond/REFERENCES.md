# The Nix of the Millpond: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Nixe im Teich_ (KHM 181) as a study of a debt that falls on the innocent and the patient faith that redeems it. The production models a miller's rash bargain with the water-spirit, the cursed son drawn under at a careless touch of the pond, and the wife who summons him back piece by piece across three full moons with three golden gifts, then keeps faith through transformation, separation, and years of shepherding until a remembered tune reunites them.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 181, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                          |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 181 | The canonical tale of the nix, the promised son, and the moon. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Hydrology of the millpond**: the pond is treated as a topological boundary between the human world and the nix's claim, ignoring depth, current, or volume of the flood.
- **Mechanism of transformation**: the becoming of toad and frog is modeled as an instantaneous state change for survival, not as biological metamorphosis.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
