# Der gescheite Hans: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der gescheite Hans_ (KHM 032) as a tragicomedy of literal translation and foolish behavior: a boy who takes every advice of his mother literally out of context, ruining his gifts and finally casting calves' eyes at his bride, driving her away.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 032, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                               |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 032 | The canonical tale of Hans's foolish literalism and his lost bride. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Intentional Stupidity**: Hans's behavior is modeled as the consequence of strict literalism, treating instructions as independent formulas without situational awareness.
- **Tragic Comic End**: the loss of the bride is the inevitable outcome of his inability to comprehend social covenants.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
