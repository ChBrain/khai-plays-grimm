# The Hand with the Knife: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Hand mit dem Messer_ (KHM 8a, withdrawn after the first edition) as a scheme of help undone by suspicion: a disfavoured girl is given a magic knife by her elf-lover's hand from a hill, until her brothers, set on by the mother, seize the knife and maim the helper with his own blade, so that he believes himself betrayed and vanishes. The production models the tale as the mechanism by which the one help a slighted child finds is destroyed by her own household. The blunt tool, the magic knife, and the severed arm are the load-bearing instruments of that design.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, first edition (1812), tale no. 8; replaced in the second edition by _Der wunderliche Spielmann_ and absent from the 1857 edition of last hand. Recorded among the tales "aus der ersten Auflage."

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Maiming as structure, not spectacle**: the severing of the elf's arm is encoded as the turning point ([Verstümmelung](process_maiming.md)) and as the load-bearing [abgeschlagener Arm](piece_severed-arm.md), stated plainly and briefly; the production does not dwell on gore or elaborate the violence.
- **No restored helper**: the elf's withdrawal is final and the misunderstanding is never cleared; the tale's unconsoled close is kept, not softened into reunion.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
