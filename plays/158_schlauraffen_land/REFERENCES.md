# The Story of Schlauraffen Land: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das Märchen vom Schlaraffenland_ (KHM 158) as a pure lying tale: a catalogue of impossibilities recited by a boasting narrator. The production treats the absurdities literally and models how a tall tale draws its pleasure from the relentless heaping of inverted-world wonders rather than from any plot, closing not by resolution but by the arbitrary crow of a cock.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 158, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **A real narrative**: there is no plot to simulate; the tale is treated as a catalogue, and no causal arc is imposed on the impossible images.
- **Physical plausibility**: each wonder is staged exactly as stated, with no attempt to reconcile it with natural law.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
