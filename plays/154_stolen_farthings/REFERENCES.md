# The Stolen Farthings: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der gestohlene Heller_ (KHM 154) as a tender ghost tale about a small, unconfessed debt that binds a pure soul to the world. The production models how a dead child silently returns to its home until the living, recognizing the apparition, make restitution on its behalf and lay it to rest.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 154, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                              |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 154 | The canonical tale _Der gestohlene Heller_ of the returning child. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Theology of the afterlife**: the child's return is treated as a simple state of unrest tied to one unredeemed object, not as a doctrine of purgatory or judgment.
- **Mechanics of the seeing**: why only the stranger perceives the child is left as a given gift, not explained as physics or sense.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
