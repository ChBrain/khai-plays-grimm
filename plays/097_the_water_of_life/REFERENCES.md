# The Water of Life: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das Wasser des Lebens_ (KHM 097) as a heroic quest about humility, mercy, and the straight path to deliverance. The production contrasts the youngest prince's courteous obedience to the dwarf's counsel with the elder princes' haughtiness and treachery, tracing how the true rescuer is revealed not by birthright or cunning but by a faithful heart that rides straight up the golden road.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 097, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                              |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 097 | The canonical heroic tale of the dying king and the water of life. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Enchantment mechanics**: the spell on the castle and its sleepers is treated as a fixed condition of the stage, not a simulated magic system.
- **War and famine logistics**: the rescue of the three kingdoms is modeled as a single decisive intervention of sword and bread, not as battlefield or supply detail.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
