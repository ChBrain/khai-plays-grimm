# The Giant and the Tailor: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Riese und der Schneider_ (KHM 183) as an illustration of bluff and intimidation: how a poor, light-weight tailor unsettles a far stronger but credulous giant through sheer boasting, only to be undone by the very lightness he bragged about when the bent willow-branch flings him into the air.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 183, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                       |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 183 | The comic boast-tale of the tailor and the credulous giant. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Physics of the willow-branch**: the catapult is treated as a simple state transition triggered by the tailor's breath, ignoring the elasticity, mass, and trajectory of a real sprung branch.
- **The 365-day wage**: the giant's enormous wages are taken as a flat comic figure, not as an economic contract to be reconciled or paid out.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
