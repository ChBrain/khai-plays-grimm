# Hans in Luck: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Hans im Glück_ (KHM 083) as an illustration of subjective contentment set against objective value. The production models a descending chain of trades in which Hans converts a head-sized lump of gold, step by step, into a horse, a cow, a pig, a goose, and finally two worthless stones, while his happiness rises at every loss until the last burden sinks into a well.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 083, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                   |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 083 | The canonical tale of Hans's cheerful chain of ever-worsening bargains. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Market valuation**: the real worth of gold, horse, cow, pig, and goose is treated as a fixed downward sequence, not as a simulated economy of prices.
- **Animal behaviour**: the bolting horse, the milkless kicking cow, and the goose are modeled as trade triggers rather than full creature simulations.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
