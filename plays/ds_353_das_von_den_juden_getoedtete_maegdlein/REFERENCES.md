# Das von den Juden getödtete Mägdlein: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das von den Juden getödtete Mägdlein_ (DS 353) as a legend of greed, betrayal, and supernatural testimony. The production models the tale as a mechanism of exposure: a murdered child raises her hand from a river to name what was done, and the wounds of the body bleed as evidence when the killers approach. The load-bearing instrument is the rising hand — the gesture that refuses to be silent — and the blood that testifies without a court.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Deutsche Sagen_ (1816-1818), legend DS 353.

| Source             | Key Work / Event         | Scope                                                                                                  |
| ------------------ | ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Deutsche Sagen_, DS 353 | The Pforzheim legend of a girl sold by an old woman, killed, and whose body testifies to her murderers. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The blood libel**: DS 353 is a blood libel legend — a fabricated accusation that Jews ritually murdered Christian children to extract their blood. Blood libel is one of the most lethal antisemitic fabrications in European history, used for centuries to justify massacres, pogroms, and expulsions. This production refuses to reproduce that accusation. The killers are encoded solely by their structural function as [die Käufer](persona_kaeufer.md) — the buyers whose role in the mechanism is purchase-enabled murder — and no ethnic, religious, ritual, or conspiratorial identity is staged. The blood libel fabrication is named here so it cannot be silently reproduced.
- **The ritual framing**: the source presents the blood-extraction as deliberate ritual. This production encodes it as the structural process [Aderlass-Marterung](process_blutentzug.md) — the method of killing — not as evidence of any group's practice or belief.
- **The bleeding wounds as divine proof**: the cruentation (corpus delicti by bleeding) is catalogued as the tale's structural beat of supernatural testimony, not as theological or racial endorsement.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
