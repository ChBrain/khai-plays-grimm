# Cat and Mouse in Partnership: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Katze und Maus in Gesellschaft_ (KHM 002) as a single predatory scheme dressed as fellowship: the strong partner enters a pact with the weak, hoards the shared winter store, and devours the partner once the theft is exposed. The production models the tale not as a fable of friendship but as a one-sided plan, the cat's design to feast in secret and silence the only witness. The pot of fat, the false christenings, and the invented godchild names are the load-bearing instruments of that design, and the outcome is decided not by justice but by the raw difference in strength.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 002, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                              |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 002 | The canonical tale of the pact, the secret pot, and the last bite. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Softened ending**: the cat's swallowing of the mouse is the cold close of the tale, "es ist der Lauf der Welt", not blunted into a reconciliation.
- **A counter-scheme**: there is no Gegenplan. The mouse is artless and keeps house in good faith; the whole motion belongs to [der heimliche Fraß am Fetttopf](plan_pot-licking.md), the cat's plan alone.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
