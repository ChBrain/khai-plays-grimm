# How Children Played at Slaughtering: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Wie Kinder Schlachtens miteinander gespielt haben_ (KHM 22a), an early tale withdrawn from the Grimms' _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ after the first edition (1812). Children playing at slaughtering a sow set off an inquiry with grave consequences.

## Origin

The Brothers Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, first edition (1812), tale KHM 22a. An early tale withdrawn from the later editions. It opens: "Es war einmal ein Vater, der schlachtete eine Sau, das sahen seine Kinder..."

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

> [!CAUTION]
> **Sensitive Content Framing Warning**
>
> This play represents KHM 22a, a tale containing extreme domestic tragedy and child death.
>
> Under the repository's strict safety guidelines, this playhouse enforces **abstract/structural modeling only**.
>
> - No graphic elements, physical violence, or bodily harm may be represented or modeled in any Plot, Persona, Place, or Piece.
> - The narrative is structured as a game of role-imitation followed by a judicial trial of intent (drawing from the second variant of the 1812 text).
> - Staging of physical suffering or deaths is strictly prohibited.

- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
