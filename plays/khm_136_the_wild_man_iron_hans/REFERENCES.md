# Iron Hans: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Eisenhans_ (KHM 136) as a system of capture, initiation, probation, and redemption. The play models the ATU 502 classification, focusing on the capture of the wild man, the key theft, the forest refuge, the gold spring test, the gardener boy disguise, the knight's combat, the tournament apple catch, and the final redemption.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 136, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                         |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 136 | The canonical tale of the wild man Iron Hans, the prince, and his initiation. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The physics of the spring**: the gold spring's chemical or physical transformation of fingers, hair, and objects is ignored and modeled purely as a magical state change.
- **Specific warfare mechanics**: the battle scenes are modeled as shown plot elements, delegating direct tactical and historical warfare details.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
