# Der ewige Jud auf dem Matterhorn: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der ewige Jud auf dem Matterhorn_ (DS 343) as a prophecy legend in which a wandering figure announces the slow erasure of a prosperous mountain town across three visits separated by centuries. The production models the tale as a structure of time, witness, and inevitability: the prophesied desolation arrives exactly as spoken, and the glacier is the verdict.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Deutsche Sagen_ (1816-1818), legend DS 343.

| Source             | Key Work / Event         | Scope                                                                                     |
| ------------------ | ------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Deutsche Sagen_, DS 343 | The Alpine legend of a wandering figure prophesying the glacial burial of a mountain town. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The antisemitic archetype**: the "Ewige Jude" (Wandering Jew) is one of the most persistent antisemitic archetypes in European folklore, encoding Jewish identity as a curse, a punishment for rejecting Christ, and a harbinger of doom. This production refuses to reproduce that theology or that slur. The wanderer is encoded solely by his structural function as [der Wanderer](position_wanderer.md): the figure who moves through time and witnesses what others cannot. No ethnic or religious condemnation, curse-narrative, or stereotype is staged; the figure is the prophecy and the long view, never the antisemitic myth to be reproduced.
- **The name as label**: the declared source title "Der ewige Jude / Laufende Jud" is retained as source identifier only, in [persona_jude.md](persona_jude.md) and [position_wanderer.md](position_wanderer.md). It is never used as a characterisation within the production.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
