# Of the Table Napkin, the Knapsack, the Cannon Hat, and the Horn: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Von der Serviette, dem Tornister, dem Kanonenhütlein und dem Horn_ (KHM 37a) as a system of wandering, magical acquisition, and military-economic restoration. The play models the mother's early marriage to the tavern host, the three sons going forth, their receipt of magic items (napkin, knapsack, cannon-hat, horn) from an old woman, their subsequent military triumph over the host and royal forces, and the liberation of their mother.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812), KHM 37 (1st edition, 1812).

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The physical laws of matter duplication**: the napkin's creation of food and the knapsack's summoning of soldiers are treated as magical constants without mechanical explanation.
- **The logistics of military campaign**: the battles against the host and the king's armies are represented as symbolic state transformations of victory and surrender.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
