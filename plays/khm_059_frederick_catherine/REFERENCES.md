# Frederick and Catherine: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Frieder und das Katherlieschen_ (KHM 059) as a tragicomedy of domestic foolishness and literalism: Catherine who ruins the vinegar, the beer, and the gold, throws cheese down a hill, and carries a door to protect it from thieves, which they drop to frighten the robbers.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 059, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                            |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 059 | The canonical tale of Frederick and Catherine's foolish mishaps. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Domestic Incompetence**: Catherine's actions are modeled as literalist interpretations of thrift and care, leading to the destruction of all family goods.
- **Accidental Deliverance**: the retrieval of the gold is the accidental consequence of throwing the door down from the tree in terror, not of clever planning.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
