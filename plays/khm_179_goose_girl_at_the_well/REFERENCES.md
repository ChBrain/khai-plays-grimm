# The Goose-Girl at the Well: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die Gänsehirtin am Brunnen_ (KHM 179) as an illustration of humble, true love wrongly punished and patiently restored. The production models the contrast between the flattering speech of the elder daughters and the youngest princess's simple "love like salt", and traces how a benevolent enchantress tests merit, shelters the outcast, and returns her, transformed and richly rewarded, to her repentant parents.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 179, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                        |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 179 | The canonical tale of the old woman, the goose-girl, and the well of pearls. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The enchantress's metaphysics**: the old woman's supernatural power is treated as a given lever for testing and reward, not as a magic system to be explained.
- **The wedding's particulars**: the teller's closing guess that the princess and count likely marry is rendered only as mutual affection, leaving the ceremony unmodeled.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
