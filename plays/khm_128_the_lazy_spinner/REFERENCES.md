# The Lazy Spinner: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die faule Spinnerin_ (KHM 128) as a study of how cunning evasion defeats honest diligence in a domestic setting. The production models a wife who will not spin and who, through a chain of pretexts, fright, and substitution, wears down her good-natured husband until he abandons every claim to the work.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 128, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                   |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 128 | The canonical tale of the idle wife who tricks her way out of spinning. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Craft detail of spinning**: the reel, wheel, and yarn are treated as plain props of household duty, ignoring the real mechanics of spinning and winding.
- **Moral verdict on the marriage**: the production stages the contest of wills without judging the wife or pitying the husband beyond the tale's own close.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
