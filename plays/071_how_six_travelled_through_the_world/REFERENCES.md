# How Six Travelled through the World: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Sechse kommen durch die ganze Welt_ (KHM 071) as a representation of cooperative skill specialization, systemic exploitation, and power subversion. The production models a squad of six hyper-specialized characters whose individual constraints are resolved through integration. The narrative shows the transition from state-inflicted economic injustice (poor soldier's pay) to complete expropriation of the state's treasury, demonstrating how extreme capabilities bypass conventional power structures (monarchy, army, laws).

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 071, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                         |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 071 | The canonical tale of the six companions and the greedy king. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Physiological Mechanics of Powers**: the exact physical principles of how the runner detaches his leg, how the blower generates hurricane-force wind, or how the Hütchenmann creates frost are treated as axiomatic state-changes.
- **Princess's Motivation**: the princess's hatred of marrying a commoner is accepted as an initial narrative boundary condition rather than active character logic.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
