# Sinfiotli's Death: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Sinfiotli's Death_, a lay from the Brothers Grimm's _Lieder der alten Edda_. Sinfiotli is poisoned by his stepmother Queen Borghild, who offers him a deadly horn of ale.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's edition and translation of the _Lieder der alten Edda_ (1815).

| Source             | Key Work / Event               | Scope                                                |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Lieder der alten Edda_ (1815) | The heroic lay as the Grimms edited and rendered it. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The wider cycle**: the production stages this lay alone, not the surrounding Eddic cycle it belongs to.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
