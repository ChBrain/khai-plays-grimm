# The Nail: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der Nagel_ (KHM 184) as a cautionary illustration of how neglected small maintenance compounds into total loss. The production models the contrast between the merchant's false economy of haste, the stable-boy's unheeded warnings, and the horse whose soundness depends entirely on a single nail.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 184, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                   |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 184 | The cautionary tale of the merchant, the missing nail, "Hasten slowly." |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Farriery detail**: the nail, shoe, and hoof are treated as a simple chain of degradation, ignoring the metallurgy or veterinary specifics of shoeing.
- **Road and weather physics**: the journey is modeled as ordered rest-stops and a final fall, rather than full terrain or distance simulation.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
