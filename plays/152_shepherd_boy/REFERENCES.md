# The Shepherd Boy: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Das Hirtenbüblein_ (KHM 152) as an illustration of how grounded wit disarms power. The production models the contrast between the King's three impossible questions (how many drops in the sea, how many stars in the sky, how many seconds in eternity) and the Shepherd Boy's method of answering not with a number but with an equally impossible condition or an unanswerable parable.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 152, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                          |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 152 | The canonical riddle tale of the wise shepherd boy and a king. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Actual quantities**: the production never models real counts of drops, stars, or seconds; the answers are parables, not measurements.
- **Geography of the Diamond Mountain**: the mountain exists only inside the boy's third answer as an image of eternity, never as a place to be reached or surveyed.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
