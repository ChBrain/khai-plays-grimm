# The White Snake: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Die weisse Schlange_ (KHM 017) as a single scheme: a servant winning the king's daughter by surviving her deadly bridal trials. The production models the tale not as a string of lucky marvels but as one deliberate plan whose capital is mercy. Having tasted the white snake and gained the speech of animals, the servant spares fish, ants, and ravens on the road; when the proud princess sets impossible tasks to refuse him, he spends that hoarded gratitude. The ring from the sea, the mountain of millet, and the apple of life are the instruments, and the outcome is decided not by his strength but by the creatures he was kind to.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 017, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                    | Scope                                                                        |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmaerchen_, KHM 017 | The canonical tale of the white snake, the three kindnesses, and the trials. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Magic as luck**: the animals' help is not coincidence. It is the deferred return on three deliberate kindnesses, the capital the single scheme spends.
- **The princess's pride**: her impossible tasks are modeled as a genuine attempt to refuse the suitor, broken only when the apple of life softens her, not waved away.
