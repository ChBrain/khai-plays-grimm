# The Glass Coffin: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der gläserne Sarg_ (KHM 163) as an illustration of how humble courage and decency break an old enchantment laid by a spurned conjurer. The production models the rise of a poor tailor's apprentice who, guided by an enchanted stag, descends beneath a rock, unbolts a glass coffin to wake a captive maiden, raises her glass-bound castle back to full size, and frees her people from smoke-bottles, until the whole vanished house is restored and he weds the count's daughter on the selfsame day.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 163, declared in German as _Der gläserne Sarg_, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Mechanics of the enchantment**: the magic of glass, stag, and smoke is treated as state transitions of bondage and release, not as a physical or chemical system.
- **Genealogy of the count's house**: the rank and lineage of the maiden and her brother are taken as given context, not modeled in detail.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
