# Prince Swan: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Prinz Schwan_ (KHM 59a) as a system of faith, trial, and restoration. The play models the ATU 425A classification (The Animal as Bridegroom), focusing on the ball of yarn, the breaking of the thread on a thorn bush, the sheltering with the personified elements (Sun, Moon, Star) while dodging the cannibal husband, the golden gifts (spinning wheel, spindle, reel), the Glass Mountain guarded by a dragon and lion pacified with bread and bacon, the false queen, the sleeping potion, the warning of the King, the riddle of the old and new key, and the reclamation of the true bride Julian'.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, first edition (1812), Band 1, tale No. 59; omitted from all editions after 1819 and absent from the 1857 edition of last hand. Oral source: Margarete "Gretchen" Wild. Tale type ATU 425A (The Animal as Bridegroom). Text: `de.wikisource.org/wiki/Prinz_Schwan_(1812)`.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The physics of a flying swan pulling a human**: the mechanical aerodynamics of Julian' following a flying swan via a thread of yarn are ignored.
- **The astronomy of the celestial bodies**: the houses of the Sun, Moon, and Stars are treated as simple forest cottages inhabited by personified elemental hosts.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
