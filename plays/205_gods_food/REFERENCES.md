# God's Food: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Gottes Speise_ (KHM 205) as a children's legend of hard-heartedness, hunger, and divine mercy. The production models a starving widow with five children who begs her rich but childless sister for bread and is harshly refused; later the rich sister's husband cuts a loaf that bleeds red blood and rushes too late to the widow's room, where three children have already died of hunger, the widow refuses earthly food because God has stilled their hunger, and at last the two youngest and the widow herself die.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 205, the children's legend known in German as _Gottes Speise_, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The theology of the miracle**: the bleeding loaf and God's stilling of hunger are staged as narrative signs, not as doctrinal claims to be argued or explained.
- **Material detail of bread and grain**: the loaf and the withheld grain are treated as plain narrative tokens, not as objects with baking or agricultural properties.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
