# The Young Giant: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging _Der junge Riese_ (KHM 090) as an illustration of raw, self-sufficient capability set against exploitative authority. The production models how a hero grown to overwhelming strength performs every task single-handed, demands payment only in two blows of his fist, and survives each treacherous attempt to cheat him until the broken bargain is settled by unavoidable retribution.

## Origin

Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_ (1812-1857), tale KHM 090, in the definitive 7th edition of 1857.

| Source             | Key Work / Event                   | Scope                                                                  |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Brothers Grimm** | _Kinder- und Hausmärchen_, KHM 090 | The canonical tale "Der junge Riese" of the thumb-sized boy made huge. |

## Restrictions

What the production refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Metallurgy of the staffs**: every iron staff and the final bar are treated as objects that simply snap under the hero's grip, ignoring true material strength or forging detail.
- **Physics of the haunted mill**: the unseen spirits are modeled as an invisible adversary trading blows in the dark, not as a simulated supernatural mechanism.
- **Cross-play context**: strict local isolation. Every link stays within this play's directory.
