# PRODUCTION_arme_heinrich.md, the Der arme Heinrich run

The plan for the Grimm house: stage Hartmann von Aue's Middle High German poem _Der arme Heinrich_ (1815 edition by the Brothers Grimm) as a **single dedicated play**, representing the courtly, religious, and moral contracts of the medieval legend.

> Source: Jacob and Wilhelm Grimm's edition of Hartmann von Aue's _Der arme Heinrich_ (1815), which is in the public domain. The prose descriptions, staging notes, and character projections follow the authentic Grimm-Deutsch translation and comment conventions.

---

## The strategy: A Single Sühnedrama

Unlike the episodic anthologies of KHM, DS, and AE, _Der arme Heinrich_ (HA) is a single, continuous narrative epic.

What this buys us:

- **Focused Moral Arc**: Staging this work as a single play captures Heinrich's fall into leprosy, his prideful despair, the pure maiden's sacrifice, and his final spiritual redemption and cure.
- **Symbolic Staging**: Every scene highlights the essential load-bearing pieces (the diagnosis, the sacrifice altar, the miraculous healing) to track the moral transformation.
- **Bilingual Structure**: The framework schema and frontmatter keys are in English, while the prose descriptions, dialogue, and cues use authentic Grimm-Deutsch.
