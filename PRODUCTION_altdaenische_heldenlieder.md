# PRODUCTION_altdaenische_heldenlieder.md, the Altdänische Heldenlieder run

The plan for the Grimm house: stage Wilhelm Grimm's translation of _Altdänische Heldenlieder, Balladen und Märchen_ (1811) as **5 ballad plays**, each representing the rhythmic, heroic, and tragic contracts of Nordic folk poetry.

> Source: Wilhelm Grimm's _Altdänische Heldenlieder, Balladen und Märchen_ (1811), which is in the public domain. The prose descriptions, staging notes, and character projections follow the authentic Grimm-Deutsch translation and comment conventions.

---

## The strategy: 5 Ballad Plays

Unlike the prose narratives of KHM and DS, the _Altdänische Heldenlieder_ (AH) season models the **fate-bound, heroic rhythm of traditional folk ballads**.

What this buys us:

- **Rhythmic Action**: Every play transitions through distinct ballad strophes, where actions are paced by tragic refrains, warnings, and the clash of iron.
- **Nordic Heroism**: Staging these plays models heroic oaths, kinship betrayals, and dragon-slaying trials under the house's strict causal rules.
- **Bilingual Structure**: The framework schema and frontmatter keys are in English, while the prose descriptions, dialogue, and cues use authentic Grimm-Deutsch.

---

## The build: one play, full Mode B

Every ballad-play is built as a play directory (`ah_NNN_`) containing:

- `play_*.md`: The play definition outlining the ballad's ENACTS container.
- `plot_NN_*.md`: The CAST movements of the ballad's narrative progression.
- `persona_*.md`, `place_*.md`, `process_*.md`, `piece_*.md`, `position_*.md`, `pitch_*.md`: The company assets.
